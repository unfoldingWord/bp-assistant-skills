# UST Transformation Patterns

This document catalogs the transformations applied when converting Hebrew/ULT to UST (meaning-based translation).

## Philosophy

UST expresses **meaning** clearly, while ULT preserves **form**. The UST should be natural English that communicates the same meaning as the Hebrew text, making implicit information explicit when helpful.

---

## 1. Idiom Transformations

Hebrew idioms are **explained** in UST rather than preserved literally.

| Hebrew Idiom | ULT (literal) | UST (meaning) |
|--------------|---------------|---------------|
| אֶרֶךְ אַפַּיִם (erek appayim) | "long of nostrils" | "does not quickly become angry" |
| לִפְנֵי (liphnei) | "to the face of" | "with" / "in the presence of" |
| בְּעֵינֵי (be'enei) | "in the eyes of" | "in the opinion of" |
| בְּיַד (beyad) | "by the hand of" | "through" / "by means of" |
| מִפִּי (mippi) | "from the mouth of" | "from" |
| עַל־לֵב (al-lev) | "on the heart" | "in mind" |
| קְשֵׁה עֹרֶף (qesheh oref) | "hard of neck" | "stubborn" |
| בֹּא בַּיָּמִים (bo bayyamim) | "come into days" | "very old" |
| יָדַע (yada) + person | "knew her" | "have sexual relations with" |
| לְ⁠פִי־ חָ֑רֶב | "to the mouth of the sword" | "completely destroy" |

### Body-Part Idioms

| Body Part | ULT | UST |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| nostrils burning | "his nose burned" | "he became very angry" |
| face | "to his face" | "in his presence" |
| hand | "by the hand of" | "through" |
| bosom | "lie in your bosom" | "sleep close to you" |
| loins | "gird your loins" | "prepare yourself" |

---

## 2. Abstract Noun Transformations

UST may convert abstract nouns to verbal or clausal forms for clarity.

| ULT (nominal) | UST (verbal/clausal) |
|---------------|---------------------|
| "the happiness of the man" | "What a good life the man has" |
| "the salvation of Yahweh" | "that Yahweh saves" |
| "covenant faithfulness" | "always faithful to his covenant" |
| "the keeping of Yahweh" | "what Yahweh commands" |

---

## 3. Metaphor Transformations

UST expresses the **meaning** of metaphors rather than the image.

| Hebrew Metaphor | ULT | UST |
|-----------------|-----|-----|
| "a land flowing with milk and honey" | literal | "a very productive land" |
| "the way of all the earth" | "the way of all the earth" | "about to die" |
| "whored after other gods" | "whored after other gods" | "were unfaithful to Yahweh and worshiped false gods" |
| "mountains quake / hills melt" | literal | "the mountains quake / the hills melt" (can keep if meaning is clear) |

---

## 4. Voice Transformations

UST prefers **active voice** over passive.

| ULT (passive) | UST (active) |
|---------------|--------------|
| "A psalm written by David" | "David wrote this song" |
| "it was told to Solomon" | "Someone told Solomon" |
| "this law was given through Moses" | "God gave this law through Moses" |

### Superscription Pattern

| Context | ULT | UST |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Circumstances stated | "A psalm of David when he fled..." | "David wrote this song when he was fleeing..." |
| No circumstances | "A psalm of David" | "A psalm of David" (keep as-is) |

---

## 5. Word Order Naturalizations

UST uses natural English word order.

| ULT (Hebrew order) | UST (natural English) |
|--------------------|-----------------------|
| "And she said to him" | "She said to him" (no initial conjunction) |
| "To me is Gilead" | "Gilead belongs to me" |
| "Beautiful to abundance was the young woman" | "The young woman was very beautiful" |

---

## 6. Conjunction Handling

UST **does not** start sentences with conjunctions like "And" or "But."

| ULT | UST |
|-----|-----|
| "And his servants said" | "So his officials said" |
| "And they sought" | "They searched" |
| "But he did not call" | "However, he did not invite" |

Use logical connectors when needed: "So", "However", "Therefore", "Then", "Meanwhile"

---

## 7. Title and Address Transformations

| ULT | UST |
|-----|-----|
| "my lord the king" | "Your Majesty" |
| "your female servant" | (context dependent) |
| "the sons of Israel" | "Israelites" |
| "the sons of Ammon" | "the Ammonites" |
| "sons of [person]" | "descendants of [person]" |

---

## 8. Implicit Information

UST makes implicit information **explicit** using {brackets}.

### When to Add

- Location clarification: "Elkosh" -> "the village of Elkosh"
- Category identification: "Nineveh" -> "the city of Nineveh"
- Contextual clarification: "they searched" -> "{The king gave them permission,} so they searched"
- Meaning clarification: "he will certainly punish" -> "he will certainly punish {those who have done evil things}"

### Length Limits

- Should not be as long as a regular sentence
- Can be a short complete sentence if reworking would be longer or change focus

### When NOT to Use Brackets

Brackets are only for content that is truly absent from the source text and essential for comprehension. Do NOT bracket:

| Category | Example (Wrong) | Example (Correct) |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Logical connectors | `{because} you answer` | `Because you answer` |
| Temporal connectors | `{when we} pray` | `when we pray` |
| Obvious context | `waves crashing {on the shore}` | `waves crashing on the shore` |
| Relationship markers | `sinned {against you}` | `sinned against you` |
| Figure of speech signals | `{It is as though} the hills rejoice` | `It is as if the hills are celebrating` |
| Natural clarifications | `Flocks {of sheep and goats}` | `Flocks of sheep and goats` |
| Category markers for well-known items | `{the city of} Nineveh` | `the city of Nineveh` |

**Principle:** If natural English expression makes the meaning clear, don't bracket it. Brackets draw attention and signal "this isn't in the source" - use them sparingly. The UST should read as flowing prose, not as a text annotated with implied content markers.

**Test:** Before adding brackets, ask: "Would a native English speaker notice this is 'added' information?" If not, just write it naturally.

## 9. Number and Plural Handling

| ULT | UST |
|-----|-----|
| "the Hittite, the Amorite" (singular) | "the Hittites, the Amorites" (plural) |
| "peoples" | "peoples" (keep as plural) |
| "nations" or "the peoples" | may use "people groups" |

---

## 10. Proper Names

UST uses the **same proper names** as ULT.

- No modern equivalents: "the Great Sea" (not "the Mediterranean Sea")
- No anachronistic names
- Same spelling for same people/places throughout Bible
- UST may add category: "the Jordan" -> "the Jordan River"
- Background info in brackets: "the Sea of Reeds {people also call it the Red Sea}"

---

## 11. Construct Chain Transformations

| ULT (construct) | UST |
|-----------------|-----|
| "the servant of Yahweh" (appositive) | "Yahweh's servant" (possessive) |
| "the son of X" | "X's son" or "son of X" (patronymic style) |
| "the border of the sons of Ammon" | "the border of the region where the Ammonites live" |

---

## 12. Special Terms

| Hebrew | ULT | UST |
|--------|-----|-----|
| אַשְׁרֵי | "The happiness of" | "What a good life..." |
| סֶלָה | "Selah" | "Selah" (keep same) |
| יהוה | "Yahweh" | "Yahweh" |
| שׁוֹפָר | "shofar" | "horn" (first: "ram's-horn trumpet") |
| מַלְאַךְ יהוה | "the angel of Yahweh" | "an angel representing Yahweh" |
| מִדְבָּר | "wilderness" | "desolate area" / "dry place" / "uninhabited region" |
| חָרַם (Hiphil) | "devote" | "destroy completely" / "set apart to belong only to Yahweh" |
| תּוֹרָה | "law" | "instruction" |

---

## 13. Measurement and Time

| ULT | UST |
|-----|-----|
| "days of X" | "throughout the lifetime of X" |
| "all the days of Joshua" | "throughout the lifetime of Joshua" |
| Technical measures | May explain meaning in context |

---

## 14. Discourse Markers

| ULT | UST Handling |
|-----|--------------|
| "Behold" | "Listen" / "Pay attention" / reword naturally |
| "And it happened" | "One day" / "Then" / natural narrative |
| "saying" (quote margin) | May restructure or omit if clear |

---

## 15. Parallelism in Poetry

UST can:
- Vary the order of parallel terms (e.g., "be glad and rejoice" can become "rejoice and be glad")
- Retain both terms even if varying order
- Use synonyms that flow more naturally in English

---

## Quick Reference: ULT vs UST Philosophy

| Aspect | ULT | UST |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Primary goal | Show Hebrew form | Communicate meaning |
| Idioms | Preserve literally | Explain meaning |
| Word order | Preserve Hebrew | Natural English |
| Conjunctions | Can start sentences | Should NOT start sentences |
| Voice | May use passive | Prefer active |
| Vocabulary | Formal, technical | Clear, common |
| {brackets} | Grammar words | Background/implicit info |
| Metaphors | Preserve | Express meaning |
| Names | Same as Hebrew | Same names + clarification |

# figs-youcrowd

## Purpose
Identify when a speaker uses singular pronouns while addressing a group/crowd, helping translators decide whether to use singular or plural forms.

## Definition (from Translation Academy)
Sometimes speakers in the Bible used the **singular** form of "you" even though they were speaking to a **group** of people. This was a feature of Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek that may not translate naturally into other languages.

The TA article also covers:
- "He" used to refer to groups (nations, tribes)
- "I" used when speaking as part of a group

## When to Use figs-youcrowd

Use this issue when:
1. **Singular "you/your" addresses multiple people** - The speaker is teaching/speaking to a crowd but uses singular pronouns to address individual situations
2. **Languages might find singular-to-crowd unnatural** - Some languages require plural pronouns when addressing groups

## Confirmed Classifications

### USE figs-youcrowd when:

1. **Jesus teaching crowds with singular "you"**
   - Sermon on the Mount (Matthew 5-7) is the classic example
   - "Even though Jesus is speaking to many disciples, he is addressing an individual situation, so **you** is singular"

2. **Individual situations applied to groups**
   - "Give to the one who asks you" - singular "you" but applies to all listeners
   - Commands for individual behavior taught to crowds

3. **Singular pronouns in epistles addressing churches**
   - Paul using "you" singular to address the church at Rome
   - Pattern: rhetorical device of addressing the general through the specific

4. **OT singular addressing collective Israel**
   - "You shall not murder" - singular command to nation
   - "You must have no other gods before me" - singular to all Israel

### Key Pattern in Notes:
The standard formula is:
> "Even though [speaker] is speaking to many [audience], he is addressing an individual situation, so **you** is singular throughout this verse. But if the singular form would not be natural in your language for someone who is speaking to a group of people, you could use the plural form of **you** in your translation."

## NOT This Issue (Use Instead)

| Situation | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Plural "you" to a group | figs-yousingular |
| Singular "you" to one person | figs-yousingular |
| Exactly two people addressed | figs-youdual |
| Formal/informal register | figs-youformal |
| "He" referring to a nation | May still be figs-youcrowd per TA |

## Recognition Process

```
Is a speaker addressing a crowd/group?
|
+--> Does the speaker use SINGULAR pronouns?
     |
     +--> YES - Singular "you" to multiple people
     |    --> Use figs-youcrowd
     |
     +--> NO - Plural "you" matches plural audience
          --> No figs-youcrowd note needed
          --> May need figs-yousingular if number needs clarification
```

## Common Patterns from Published Notes

1. **Matthew 5-7 (Sermon on the Mount)** - Highest concentration
   - 27 occurrences in Matthew
   - Nearly every teaching uses singular-to-crowd

2. **Romans** - Second highest (19 occurrences)
   - Paul's diatribe style: singular "you" addressing Roman church
   - Romans 2:17-27: Extended singular address to hypothetical Jew

3. **Luke** - Common in Jesus' teachings
   - Lord's Prayer teaching
   - Parables applied to audiences

4. **Exodus/Leviticus** - Singular commands to Israel
   - Ten Commandments
   - Holiness Code

## Alternate Translations

Alternate translations for figs-youcrowd typically:
- Offer the plural alternative: "you all" / "each of you"
- Sometimes not needed if the note explains the option clearly

## Key Distinction from Related Issues

| Issue | When "you" is... | Addressing... |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| figs-youcrowd | SINGULAR | CROWD/GROUP |
| figs-yousingular | plural OR singular | matches audience size |
| figs-youdual | dual | exactly 2 people |

## Biblical Examples

**Matthew 6:2** (ULT)
> "So when **you** give alms, do not sound a trumpet before **yourself**"

Jesus speaks to the crowd but uses singular. Note: "Even though Jesus is speaking to many disciples, he is addressing an individual situation, so **you** and **yourself** are singular."

**Exodus 20:3** (ULT)
> "**You** must have no other gods before me."

God speaks to all Israel using singular. This addresses the nation through each individual.

# grammar-connect-words-phrases

## Purpose
Identify connecting words that function unexpectedly, signal structural transitions, or have ambiguous relationships to surrounding text.

---

## WHEN TO WRITE A NOTE

This is a **catch-all category** for connecting words that don't fit the more specific connection types. Only write a note when:

1. **The connector functions unexpectedly** - "But" doesn't indicate contrast, "For" doesn't give a reason, "And" introduces something unexpected
2. **Structural transition** - The connector signals a new section, topic shift, or narrative development
3. **The relationship is ambiguous** - The connector could be understood multiple ways
4. **Non-standard usage** - The connecting word has a meaning different from its typical usage

Do NOT write notes for:
- Standard connectors with clear, obvious function
- Connections covered by more specific issue types (see below)

---

## Definition

From Translation Academy:
> Connecting words and phrases show how thoughts are related to each other... They can connect phrases or clauses within a sentence. They can connect sentences to each other. They can also connect entire chunks to one another in order to show how the chunk before relates to the chunk after the connecting word.

This issue addresses general connecting words that need clarification but don't fit the specific grammar-connect subcategories.

---

## CRITICAL DISTINCTION: When to Use Specific Issue Types Instead

**grammar-connect-words-phrases is the GENERAL category**. Use these SPECIFIC issues when applicable:

| Specific Issue | Use When | Key Signal |
|----------------|----------|------------|
| grammar-connect-logic-result | Reason-result relationship (cause-effect) | for, because, therefore, so that |
| grammar-connect-logic-goal | Purpose relationship (intention) | so that, in order to, lest |
| grammar-connect-logic-contrast | Two things being contrasted | but, however, yet |
| grammar-connect-exceptions | One item excluded from a group | except, unless, apart from |
| grammar-connect-time-sequential | Events in sequence | then, afterward, after |
| grammar-connect-time-simultaneous | Events at same time | while, as, during |
| grammar-connect-time-background | Ongoing background for main event | while + imperfect |
| grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical | Conditional if/then | if, when (conditional) |
| grammar-connect-condition-fact | "If" meaning "since" | if (= since) |
| grammar-connect-condition-contrary | Counterfactual condition | if (contrary to fact) |

**Use grammar-connect-words-phrases when**:
- The connector doesn't fit the categories above
- The connector signals a STRUCTURAL transition (new section, topic shift)
- The connector's function is NON-STANDARD for that word

## Categories of Connecting Word Issues

### 1. Structural Transition Markers

Words that signal movement in the text structure - new sections, topics, or argumentative steps. Common words: Now, But, And, Then

| Reference | Text | Function | AT |
|-----------|------|----------|-----|
| Matthew 1:18 | "**Now** the birth of Jesus Christ..." | Introduces next major event | [Then,] or untranslated |
| Romans 2:17 | "**But** if you call yourself a Jew..." | Transitions to new audience/topic | [However] |
| 1 Corinthians 1:10 | "**Now** I urge" | New section after thanksgiving | [Next,] or untranslated |
| Hebrews 5:1 | "**For** every high priest..." | Signals new section | [Now] or untranslated |

### 2. Explanatory "For" (Not Reason-Result)

"For" that introduces EXPLANATION or ELABORATION rather than a reason for what came before.

| Reference | Text | Function | AT |
|-----------|------|----------|-----|
| 1 Kings 1:25 | "**For** he has gone down today" | Explains what made Nathan ask | [You see,] or [After all,] |
| 1 Kings 2:26 | "**For** you carried the ark" | Assertion, not reason | [Truly] |
| Matthew 3:3 | "**For** this is he who was spoken of" | Further explanation | [In fact,] or [Now] |
| 1 Samuel 16:7 | "**for** man looks at the outward appearance" | Explains contrast | [Specifically,] |

### 3. Non-Contrastive "But"

"But" that does NOT indicate contrast but signals development or next step.

| Reference | Text | Function | AT |
|-----------|------|----------|-----|
| Matthew 6:27 | "**But** who of you by worrying can add...?" | Development, not contrast | [Indeed,] or [Further,] |
| Matthew 6:30 | "**But** if God so clothes the grass" | Development in argument | [Indeed,] or [Further,] |
| Mark 5:34 | "**But** he said to her..." | Next thing that happened | [Then] |

### 4. "And" Introducing Unexpected Result or Contrast

"And" that connects to something unexpected or functions like "but."

| Reference | Text | Function | AT |
|-----------|------|----------|-----|
| Matthew 1:25 | "**And** he did not know her" | Unexpected for married couple | [But] or [Despite that,] |
| Mark 4:17 | "**and** immediately they fall away" | Contrast with initial reception | [but] |
| Mark 5:4 | "**and** he had broken the chains" | Contrast with being tied up | [but] |

### 5. "Or" Introducing Related Questions or Alternatives

"Or" that introduces similar situations, not true alternatives.

| Reference | Text | Function | AT |
|-----------|------|----------|-----|
| Matthew 7:4 | "**Or** how will you say...?" | Another improper behavior | [Again,] or [Even further,] |
| Matthew 7:9 | "**Or** what man is there...?" | Contrasting alternative | [Indeed,] or [As a matter of fact,] |
| Mark 4:30 | "**or** with what parable shall we present it?" | Related question | [and] or [or again,] |

### 6. "And now" / "But now" Idiom

Hebrew/Greek expression introducing main point or topic.

| Reference | Text | Function | AT |
|-----------|------|----------|-----|
| 1 Kings 1:12 | "**And now**" | Introduces main topic | [So this is what I want to say:] |
| 1 Kings 1:18 | "**But now**" | Contrast + main point | Same with contrastive element |
| 1 Samuel 6:7 | "**And now**" | Main point after background | [Now this is the main thing:] |

### 7. Connecting Word Clarifying Relationship

When a connector shows how two things relate but needs explicit statement.

| Reference | Text | Function | AT |
|-----------|------|----------|-----|
| Galatians 2:20 | "**that** {is in the Son of God" | Specifies object of faith | [namely, in] or [which is in] |
| Romans 1:32 | "**that** those who practice such things" | Content of decree | [namely, that] |
| 1 Kings 7:12 | "**and** for the inner courtyard" | Comparison, not addition | [just as for] |

### 8. "Again" / "Similarly" Introducing Parallel Example

Words introducing another example of the same pattern.

| Reference | Text | Function | AT |
|-----------|------|----------|-----|
| Matthew 5:33 | "**Again**, you have heard" | New group of contrasts | [Further] or [Even more] |
| Matthew 5:30 | "**And** if your right hand..." | Another parallel example | [Again,] or [Here is another example:] |
| Mark 4:16 | "**And similarly**" | Similar experience | [Likewise] or [In a similar way] |

## NOT grammar-connect-words-phrases (Use Specific Issues)

| Expression | Issue to Use | Why |
|------------|--------------|-----|
| "**because** he did X" | grammar-connect-logic-result | Reason-result |
| "**so that** he might" | grammar-connect-logic-goal | Purpose |
| "X **but** not Y" (true contrast) | grammar-connect-logic-contrast | Real contrast |
| "**except** for" | grammar-connect-exceptions | Exception |
| "**then** he did Y" (sequence) | grammar-connect-time-sequential | Time sequence |
| "**while** he was" | grammar-connect-time-simultaneous | Simultaneous |
| "**if** X **then** Y" | grammar-connect-condition-* | Conditional |

## Recognition Process

```
Is there a connecting word (And, But, For, Now, Or, etc.)?
  |
  +-- Does it fit a SPECIFIC grammar-connect issue?
  |     |
  |     +-- YES --> Use the specific issue
  |     |
  |     +-- NO: Is there something NOTABLE about this connector?
  |           |
  |           +-- Signals structural transition (new section/topic)?
  |           |     --> grammar-connect-words-phrases
  |           |
  |           +-- Functions UNLIKE its typical usage?
  |           |     --> grammar-connect-words-phrases
  |           |
  |           +-- Relationship is AMBIGUOUS?
  |           |     --> grammar-connect-words-phrases
  |           |
  |           +-- Connection is standard and clear?
  |                 --> No note needed
```

---

## Common Patterns in Published Notes

| Pattern | Template |
|---------|----------|
| Structural "Now"/"But" | Here, the word **Now** introduces the next major event. AT: [Then,] or leave untranslated |
| Explanatory "For" | Here the word **For** introduces not a reason but an explanation. AT: [After all] |
| Non-contrastive "But" | Here, the word **But** introduces a development, not a contrast. AT: [Indeed,] or [Further,] |
| "And" unexpected | Here, the word **and** introduces something unexpected. AT: [But] or [Despite that,] |
| "And now" idiom | **And now** was an expression used to introduce the main topic. Your language may have a comparable expression. |

---

## Summary Decision Guide

1. First, check if a SPECIFIC grammar-connect issue applies (logic, time, condition, etc.)
2. If not, ask: Is there something NOTABLE about this connector?
3. Notable = structural transition, non-standard function, or ambiguous relationship
4. If standard and clear, no note needed

**Key insight**: This is the catch-all for connectors that don't fit specific subcategories but still need explanation for translators.

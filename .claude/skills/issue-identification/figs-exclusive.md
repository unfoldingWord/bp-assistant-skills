# figs-exclusive

## Purpose
Identify when first-person plural pronouns (we, us, our) need clarification about whether they include or exclude the addressee.

## Definition
Some languages have two forms of "we/us/our":
- **Inclusive**: includes the person being spoken to ("I and you")
- **Exclusive**: excludes the person being spoken to ("I and someone else, but not you")

Hebrew, Aramaic, Greek, and English do not mark this distinction, but many target languages do. Translators need to know which form to use.

## Key Decision (Issues Resolved Nov 12, 2025)
Figs-exclusive notes should say whether the biblical speaker or writer **would have used** an exclusive or inclusive form if his language had had those forms. Notes address how the speaker/writer would have addressed his **original audience** - NOT which parts of a group were included or how the text addresses modern readers.

## Recognition Triggers

A figs-exclusive note is needed when:
1. A speaker/writer uses "we," "us," or "our"
2. The pronoun reference group is not immediately obvious
3. Target language translators need to choose between inclusive/exclusive forms

## Determining Exclusive vs Inclusive

### Exclusive (speaker + others, NOT the addressee)
Use when the speaker is talking about a group that does NOT include the person being spoken to:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Author/apostle + fellow preachers (not recipients) | Paul: "we have heard" (1 John 1:1-2) - apostles, not the readers |
| Speaker + companions (not the one addressed) | Disciples to Jesus: "we are perishing" - wait, this is INCLUSIVE since Jesus is with them |
| Group member + group (not outsider being addressed) | Jews speaking of "our nation" to non-Jews |
| "We" in epistolary opening referring to senders only | "we send greetings" - senders, not recipients |

### Inclusive (speaker + addressee + possibly others)
Use when the speaker includes the person being spoken to:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Shepherds speaking to each other | "Let us go to Bethlehem" (Luke 2:15) |
| Jesus including disciples he speaks to | "Let us go to the other side" (Luke 8:22) |
| "Our God" / "our Father" in epistles to believers | Paul and the Galatians share the same God |
| Group addressing itself | "our streets" when speaking within the community |

## Common Contexts

### Epistles (most common)
- **"Our Father/God/Lord"** - typically INCLUSIVE (writer + readers worship same God)
- **"We preach/proclaim/testify"** - typically EXCLUSIVE (apostles, not readers)
- **"We believe/received"** - check context: sometimes inclusive (all believers), sometimes exclusive (Jewish believers, or just Paul)
- **"We sent Timothy"** - EXCLUSIVE (Paul/companions, not recipients)

### Gospels
- Disciples discussing among themselves - often INCLUSIVE
- Disciples speaking to Jesus about themselves as a group - check if Jesus is included or excluded
- Characters addressing Jesus about their own group - usually EXCLUSIVE

### Old Testament Narrative
- Characters speaking to each other within same group - usually INCLUSIVE
- Characters speaking to outsiders about their group - EXCLUSIVE
- "Let us" constructions between equals - usually INCLUSIVE

### Special Cases
- **God speaking of himself plurally** (Gen 3:22 "like one of us") - INCLUSIVE (God speaking to himself)
- **Dual pronouns** - when exactly two people, note if language has dual form

## NOT This Issue

| Situation | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "You" singular vs plural | figs-yousingular |
| "You" formal vs informal | figs-youformal |
| Exactly two people addressed | figs-youdual |
| Unclear pronoun referent (any person) | writing-pronouns |
| Singular noun for group | grammar-collectivenouns |

## Note Format

Notes do NOT typically include alternate translations (per Issues Resolved). Format:

**Exclusive:**
> By **us**, SPEAKER means himself and [others] but not those to whom he is speaking, so use the exclusive form of that word in your translation if your language marks that distinction.

**Inclusive:**
> By **us**, SPEAKER means to include both himself and those to whom he is speaking, so use the inclusive form of that word if your language marks that distinction.

**When referent needs clarification:**
> Here, **we** refers to [specific group]. It does not include [excluded party].

Or with options:
> Here, **we** could refer to: (1) [group A]. Alternate translation: [text]; (2) [group B]. Alternate translation: [text]

## Complex Examples from Published Notes

### Multiple pronouns with different references in same verse
> 1 Thessalonians 3:11: "our God" and "our Lord" (inclusive - all believers) vs "our way" (exclusive - Paul and companions, not Thessalonians)

### Shifting reference in letter
> In 1 John, "we" shifts between exclusive (John + eyewitnesses) in 1:1-5 and inclusive (John + readers) starting 4:6.

### Same verse, different forms
> Luke 9:33: "for us" (inclusive - all six people) vs "let us make" (exclusive - Peter, James, John, not Moses/Elijah)

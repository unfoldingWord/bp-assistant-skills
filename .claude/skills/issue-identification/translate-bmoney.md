# translate-bmoney

## Purpose
Identify biblical monetary units that may need translation notes.

## Definition
Biblical money evolved from weighed metals (OT) to standardized coins (NT). Early trade used silver/gold by weight; later periods used minted coins.

**Key Principle**: Do NOT use modern money values - they change over time and will make the translation outdated.

## Common Monetary Units

### Old Testament (by weight)
| Unit | Metal | Weight |
|------|-------|--------|
| daric | gold coin | 8.4 g |
| shekel | various | 11 g |
| talent | various | 33 kg |

### New Testament (by wages)
| Unit | Metal | Value |
|------|-------|-------|
| denarius | silver | 1 day's wage |
| drachma | silver | 1 day's wage |
| mite | copper | 1/64 day |
| shekel | silver | 4 days |
| talent | silver | 6,000 days |

## When to Write Notes

Write notes when these terms appear:
- **denarius/denarii** - Common NT coin (day's wage)
- **drachma** - Greek silver coin
- **talent** - Large sum (NT: ~6000 days wages)
- **mina** - Medium sum (~3 months wages)
- **shekel** (as currency, not weight)
- **daric** - Persian gold coin
- **mite** - Small copper coin

## NOT translate-bmoney

| Use This Instead | When |
|------------------|------|
| translate-bweight | Shekels/talents as WEIGHT (not currency) |
| translate-bdistance | Cubits, spans (distance measurements) |
| translate-bvolume | Ephah, hin (volume measurements) |

**Important Distinction**: In OT, shekels and talents often describe WEIGHT of metal (use translate-bweight). Only use translate-bmoney when clearly referring to monetary value/payment.

## Translation Principles (from TA)

1. **NEVER use modern currency** - becomes outdated/inaccurate
2. For OT weight-based money, see translate-bweight
3. Options for NT coins:
   - Transliterate the term ("denari")
   - Describe the metal ("silver coin")
   - Describe the value ("one day's wages")
   - Use biblical term with explanation

## Recognition Patterns

### NT Monetary Terms
- denarius/denarii - Roman silver coin
- drachma - Greek silver coin
- talent (Greek) - huge sum
- mina/minas - medium large sum
- copper coin / bronze coin

### OT Monetary Terms (when used as currency)
- "X shekels of silver/gold" in payment context
- daric/darics - Persian coins
- talent (as payment)

## Key Distinction: Weight vs. Currency

**OT shekels as WEIGHT** (use translate-bweight):
- "weighed out 400 shekels of silver" - weighing metal
- "the spear weighed 300 shekels" - physical weight

**OT/NT shekels as CURRENCY** (use translate-bmoney):
- "paid 30 shekels" - price/payment
- "owed 100 denarii" - debt/wages

## Note Approaches

### Option 1: Transliterate
Keep "denarii" and spell phonetically in target language

### Option 2: Describe Metal
"500 silver coins"

### Option 3: Describe Value
"500 days' wages" (most meaningful for readers)

### Option 4: Biblical Term + Explanation
"500 denarii" with footnote explaining value

## Examples from Published Notes

**Luke 7:41**: "500 denarii... 50 denarii"
- Notes explain: ~500 days' wages vs. ~50 days' wages

**Matthew 25:15**: "five talents... two talents... one talent"
- Notes explain: ~90 years' wages, ~35 years' wages, etc.

**Ezra 2:69**: "5000 silver minas"
- Notes explain: ~half kilogram each, suggest "silver bars" or give weight

**Luke 19:13**: "ten minas"
- Notes explain value in terms of wages

## Categories

### Small Amounts (daily transactions)
- mite, denarius, drachma
- Usually describe as "coin" or "day's wages"

### Medium Amounts (significant sums)
- mina (plural minas)
- Describe as "amount equal to X days/months wages"

### Large Amounts (wealth/debt)
- talent (singular or plural)
- Express as "X years' wages" to convey magnitude

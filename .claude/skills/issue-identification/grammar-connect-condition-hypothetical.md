# grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical

## Purpose
Identify simple conditions where the grammatical structure needs clarification (unstated "if"/"then" or clause reordering), not imaginary/unreal situations (that's figs-hypo).

## Definition
A hypothetical condition is a conditional relationship where:
- The second event ("then" clause) will only happen if the first event ("if" clause) takes place
- The condition represents a REAL POSSIBILITY (unlike figs-hypo which is imaginary)
- The grammatical structure may need clarification for readers

From Translation Academy:
> A Hypothetical Condition is a condition in which the second event (the "then" clause) will only take place if the first event (the "if" clause) takes place or is fulfilled in some way.

---

## CRITICAL DISTINCTION: grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical vs figs-hypo

These two issues are easily confused. The distinction is based on the Issues Resolved document (Aug 6, 2025):

| Issue | Use For | Key Test |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical** | SIMPLE conditions where "if" or "then" is unstated | Is it a REAL possible condition needing grammatical clarification? |
| **figs-hypo** | IMAGINARY conditions ("if I were you") | Is the situation UNREAL/impossible/contrary-to-fact? |

### When to use grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical
From Issues Resolved (Perry, Zulip):
> grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical: to clarify a simple condition where the "if" or "then" is not stated. Can also be used to reorder a "then" that is before an "if."

### When to use figs-hypo
> figs-hypo: imaginary conditions, ("if I were you")

**Quick Test**:
- Is this a REAL possible condition that could actually happen? --> grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical
- Is this IMAGINARY, impossible, or contrary to what actually happened? --> figs-hypo

## Three Primary Use Cases

### 1. Missing "If" (Condition Not Explicitly Marked)

The condition is implied but "if" is not stated.

**Pattern**: Statement appears as declaration but is actually conditional

**Examples**:
- Leviticus 13:2: "A man, **when** he has a swelling..."
  - Needs: "**If** a man has a swelling..."

- Leviticus 24:17: "**when** he strikes any life of a man"
  - Needs: "**If** anyone strikes/kills anyone..."

- Luke 10:28: "Do this, and you will live"
  - Needs: "**If** you do this, **then** you will live"

- 2 Peter 1:10: "for doing these things, you will certainly not ever stumble"
  - Needs: "**if** you do these things, **then** you will certainly not ever stumble"

### 2. Missing "Then" (Consequence Not Explicitly Marked)

The result/consequence is stated but "then" is not explicit.

**Pattern**: Condition stated, but result clause needs marking

**Examples**:
- 1 Kings 8:41-43: Condition in v.41, consequence in v.43
  - v.41: "suppose he comes..." (condition)
  - v.43: "you, may you hear" (consequence needs "then")

**Template**: "This verse introduces the consequence of the condition proposed in the previous verse. Use a natural form in your language for introducing the consequence of this possible condition. Alternate translation: [In that case,]"

### 3. Reordering Needed (Then Before If)

The consequence appears before the condition, which may confuse readers.

**Pattern**: Result clause comes first, condition comes second

**Examples**:
- "God promised to bless the people and protect them, **if** they obeyed these laws"
  - Could reorder to: "**If** the people obeyed these laws, **then** God promised to bless and protect them"

**Template**: "Moses is presenting a possible condition. Use a natural form in your language for introducing a condition that could happen. It may be helpful to reorder this sentence so that the conditional statement is first. Alternate translation: [If CONDITION, then CONSEQUENCE]"

## Categories of grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical

### 1. Legal/Case Law Conditions
Very common in Leviticus, Exodus, Deuteronomy.

**Pattern**: "A man, when..." / "Whoever..." / "When someone..."

**Examples**:
- Leviticus 13:2: "A man, when he has" --> "If a man has"
- Leviticus 13:40: "And a man, when his head is made bare" --> "If some man loses the hair"
- Leviticus 15:16: "And a man, when..." --> "If a man..."
- Leviticus 24:15: "A man, a man if he curses his God" --> "Whoever curses his God"

### 2. Conditional Promises/Warnings
The promise or warning depends on a condition being met.

**Examples**:
- Luke 10:28: "Do this, and you will live" --> "If you do this, then God will give you eternal life"
- 2 Corinthians 5:17: "if anyone is in Christ" --> indicates being in Christ leads to being a new creation

### 3. True Possibilities (May or May Not Happen)
The condition is a genuine possibility that could go either way.

**Examples**:
- 1 Corinthians 4:19: "if the Lord wills" - uncertain whether Lord will "will" or not
- 1 Corinthians 7:8: "if they remain as Paul is" - they might or might not remain
- 1 Timothy 2:15: "if they remain in..." - women might or may not remain

### 4. Instructional Conditions
Teaching about what to do IF something happens.

**Examples**:
- Luke 17:3: "If your brother sins, rebuke him" --> real possible event
- Luke 17:3b: "and if he repents, forgive him" --> another possible event

## NOT grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical (Use These Instead)

### Use figs-hypo for:
| Expression | Why NOT grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical |
|------------|------------------------------------------------|
| "If only we had died" | IMAGINARY - expressing wish/regret about past |
| "If the foot would say..." | ABSURD - body parts cannot talk |
| "If you had been here" | CONTRARY-TO-FACT - person was NOT there |
| "I would have sent you with joy" | COUNTERFACTUAL - didn't happen |
| "Suppose someone said to God..." | IMAGINARY teaching scenario |

### Use grammar-connect-condition-fact for:
| Expression | Why different |
|------------|---------------|
| "If" meaning "since" | FACTUAL condition, not hypothetical |
| "If you are the Son of God" (when true) | When condition is actually true |

### Use grammar-connect-condition-contrary for:
| Expression | Why different |
|------------|---------------|
| Counterfactual logical arguments | "If A then B; B is unacceptable; therefore not-A" |

## Do NOT Write Notes For

From Issues Resolved (Aug 6, 2025):
> We do not write notes for simple conditions, for example, "If this happens, then that will happen."

Only use grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical when:
- "If" or "then" is NOT stated and needs clarification
- Clauses need reordering for clarity
- The conditional structure is unclear

Do NOT write notes when:
- The condition is clearly marked with "if...then"
- The structure is already clear to readers

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify the condition**: Is there a conditional relationship (if X, then Y)?

2. **Check if it's a REAL possibility**:
   - Could this actually happen?
   - Is it a legal case, instruction, promise, or warning about real events?
   - If YES --> Continue to step 3
   - If NO (imaginary/contrary-to-fact) --> Use figs-hypo instead

3. **Check for grammatical issues**:
   - Is "if" missing or implicit?
   - Is "then" missing or implicit?
   - Are clauses in confusing order (then before if)?
   - If any YES --> grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical

4. **If grammar is clear**:
   - Do NOT write a note (per Issues Resolved)

# figs-synecdoche

## Purpose
Identify synecdoche: part represents whole, or whole represents part.

## Definition
Synecdoche: **part** represents **whole**, or **whole** represents **part**. Key is PART-WHOLE relationship, not association (metonymy) or comparison (metaphor).

## Recognition Test

**Substitution Test**: Can the whole substitute for the part?
- "my **soul** magnifies" -> "**I** magnify" = YES -> synecdoche
- "into your **hand**" -> "into **you**" = NO (loses "power" meaning) -> metonymy

**Part-Whole Test**: Is X literally part of Y, or just associated?
- "my soul" is PART of me -> synecdoche
- "throne" is ASSOCIATED with kingship -> metonymy

## Categories

### 1. Body Part for Person (Most Common)
| Term | Example |
|------|---------|
| soul | "My **soul** magnifies" = "I magnify" |
| heart | "My **heart** rejoices" = "I rejoice" |
| hands | "Works of my **hands**" = "my works" |
| feet | "Their **feet** run" = "they run" |
| eyes | "My **eyes** have seen" = "I have seen" |
| bones | "All my **bones** feared" = "I completely feared" |
| flesh | "All **flesh**" = "all people/creatures" |
| ear, mouth, lips | Person hearing/speaking |

### 2. Whole for Part (Representative)
| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "**The Pharisees** said" | One representative spoke |
| "**The Jews**" (John) | Jewish leaders, not all Jews |
| "**He** got into the boat" | Jesus and disciples |
| "**I** beheaded John" (Herod) | His soldiers did it |

### 3. Whole for Part (Location)
| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "into **the temple**" | Temple courtyard (priests only entered building) |
| "on **the mountains**" | One mountain |

### 4. Specific for General
| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| **bread** | Food in general |
| **sword** | All weapons |
| **naked** | Without possessions |

### 5. Part for Whole (Structure)
| Expression | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| "**gates**" (physical) | City borders |
| "four **corners**" | Structural supports |

## NOT figs-synecdoche

### Use figs-metonymy:
| Expression | Why |
|------------|-----|
| "face" expressions | Per Issues Resolved: "In general we will not use figs-synecdoche for 'face'" |
| "hand" = power/control | Cannot substitute whole person |
| "blood" = death | Associated, not part-whole |
| "throne" = authority | Associated, not part-whole |
| "gates" = community | When referring to PEOPLE (not physical boundary) |
| "name" = reputation | Associated, not part-whole |

### Use figs-idiom:
- "in the eyes of" (fixed expression)
- "from the face of" = because of (causal)

### Use figs-metaphor:
- "heart" = thoughts/feelings (comparison)
- "way/path" = behavior (life AS journey)

## Context-Dependent: "gates"

From Issues Resolved:
- "within your **gates**" (people living there) -> figs-metonymy
- "within your **gates**" (physical boundary) -> figs-synecdoche

# figs-rpronouns

## Purpose
Identify reflexive pronouns in biblical text where the same person is both subject and object of an action.

## Definition
Reflexive pronouns ("myself," "yourself," "himself," "herself," "itself," "ourselves," "yourselves," "themselves") show that the same person fills two different roles in a sentence - typically as both subject and object of the verb.

---

## CRITICAL: What figs-rpronouns Is and Is NOT

### figs-rpronouns IS FOR:
**Same person as subject AND object** - The reflexive pronoun indicates that the action is performed on oneself:
- "purify **themselves**" - people purifying their own selves
- "sells **himself**" - person selling their own self into slavery
- "exhausted **themselves**" - Egyptians exhausting their own selves
- "corrupted **themselves**" - people making their own selves corrupt

### figs-rpronouns is NOT FOR (Use writing-pronouns):
**Intensive/emphatic pronouns** - When Hebrew uses an independent personal pronoun with a verb already marked for person to add emphasis:
- "I, I will be king" - NOT "I myself will be king" (per Issues Resolved Jul 23, 2025)
- The ULT shows this as doubled pronoun, address with writing-pronouns

**Key Decision from Issues Resolved**: When an author or character uses an independent personal pronoun with a verb that is already marked for person, gender, and number, ULT shows this as "I, I will be king" rather than "I myself will be king." Address with **writing-pronouns** rather than figs-rpronouns.

---

## Two Main Uses

### 1. Reflexive: Same Person Fills Two Roles
The subject performs an action on themselves:
- "he shall **purify himself**" - he purifies his own self
- "sells **himself** to you" - becomes your slave
- "stripped **themselves**" - removed their own items
- "prostrated **himself**" - bowed his own body down

**Pattern**: Verb + reflexive pronoun where the action returns to the actor.

### 2. Solitary: By Oneself/Alone
The reflexive indicates being alone or doing something without others:
- "withdrew up the mountain **by himself**"
- "folded up in a place **by itself**"

**Pattern**: "by" + reflexive pronoun = alone, without others

---

## NOT figs-rpronouns (Use These Instead)

### Use writing-pronouns for:
| Expression | Example | Reason |
|------------|---------|--------|
| Hebrew independent pronoun + verb | "I, I will be king" | Emphasis, not reflexive action |
| Repeated pronoun for focus | "you, you know" | Focus shift, not reflexive |
| Contrast emphasis | "He, he will fight" | Contrasting roles, not self-action |

### Use figs-123person for:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| Self-reference in 3rd person | "your servant" meaning "I" (humility) |
| Addressing someone in 3rd person | "my lord" meaning "you" (respect) |

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify the pronoun**: Is there a reflexive pronoun (myself, yourself, himself, etc.)?

2. **Check if same person fills two roles**:
   - Is the subject performing the action ON themselves? -> figs-rpronouns
   - Is it "by [pronoun]" meaning alone? -> figs-rpronouns
   - Is it for emphasis without self-action? -> writing-pronouns

3. **For Hebrew text specifically**:
   - Is there a doubled pronoun (independent + verbal marking)? -> writing-pronouns
   - Is the verb reflexive (Hitpael, Niphal reflexive)? -> figs-rpronouns

---

## Common Patterns

### Hebrew Reflexive Patterns (figs-rpronouns)
- Hitpael verbs: often indicate reflexive action
- "Sells himself" (labor/slavery context)
- "Purify themselves" (ritual context)
- "Gathered themselves" (assembly context)

### Greek Reflexive Patterns (figs-rpronouns subject & object)
- heauton/heautous + verb where subject acts on self
- But Greek often uses reflexive forms for emphasis too - check context

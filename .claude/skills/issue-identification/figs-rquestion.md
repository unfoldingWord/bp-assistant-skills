# figs-rquestion

## Purpose
Identify rhetorical questions - questions used for purposes other than seeking information.

## Definition
A rhetorical question is a question where the speaker is more interested in expressing an attitude or making a point than in getting information. Key characteristics:
- Speaker already knows the answer (or no answer exists)
- Speaker does not expect an answer
- Purpose is to express emotion, rebuke, remind, teach, or emphasize

From TA: "A rhetorical question is a question that strongly expresses the speaker's attitude toward something. Often the speaker is not looking for information at all."

## NOT figs-rquestion

### Genuine Information-Seeking Questions
| Question Type | Reason | Example |
|---------------|--------|---------|
| Questions where speaker doesn't know | Seeking actual information | "Where are you?" (when lost) |
| Questions expecting a specific answer | Actual inquiry | "What is your name?" |
| Questions in dialogue awaiting response | Conversational exchange | Back-and-forth discussions |

**Test**: If the speaker genuinely does not know the answer and expects information in return, it is NOT a rhetorical question.

### Use figs-irony when:
- The question contains sarcastic or ironic content
- The speaker means the opposite of what the question implies
- Example: "Are you saying to kill me?" (sarcastic threat) - tag BOTH figs-rquestion AND figs-irony

### Use figs-declarative when:
- A statement is used to make a request or command
- Not a question form at all

## Recognition Process

1. **Is it a question?** Check for question form (interrogative words, inverted word order, question marks).

2. **Apply the rhetorical question test**:
   - Does the speaker already know the answer?
   - Does the speaker expect NO answer or expect the hearer to answer silently?
   - Is the purpose to express emotion, rebuke, teach, or emphasize rather than get information?

3. **Identify the purpose**: Match to one of the categories above.

4. **Check for combined figures**:
   - Questions can contain irony - tag both figs-rquestion and figs-irony if the content is sarcastic
   - Questions can contain other figures (metaphor, metonymy) - note separately

## Key Distinctions

| Distinction | Example | Classification |
|-------------|---------|----------------|
| God knows answer, giving chance to confess | "Where is Abel your brother?" | RHETORICAL |
| Genuinely asking for information | "Where did you put him?" (Lazarus tomb) | NOT rhetorical |

**Multiple questions in sequence**: Common in poetry and Paul's letters; each question gets its own note.

**Implied answers**: Many rhetorical questions expect specific answers:
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Is anything too hard for Yahweh?" | "No, nothing is too hard" |
| "Will a virgin forget her jewelry?" | "Of course not" |
| "Who makes you superior?" | "No one" |

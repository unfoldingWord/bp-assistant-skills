# figs-nominaladj

## Purpose
Identify adjectives used as nouns in biblical text to refer to a class of people or things.

## Definition
A nominal adjective is an adjective used as a noun to refer to a class of people or things that the adjective describes. For example, "the righteous" means "righteous people" and "the poor" means "poor people."

---

## CRITICAL: What figs-nominaladj Is and Is NOT

### figs-nominaladj IS FOR:
**Adjectives functioning as nouns** to represent people or things with that characteristic:
- "**the righteous**" = righteous people
- "**the wicked**" = wicked people
- "**the poor**" = poor people
- "**the meek**" = meek people
- "**the blind**" = blind people
- "**many**" = many people
- "**all**" = all people/everyone
- "**the good**" = good things
- "**the holy**" = holy things

### figs-nominaladj is NOT FOR:
| Expression | Use Instead | Reason |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| "the house of Israel" | figs-metaphor | Noun "house" used metaphorically for descendants |
| "sons of valor" | figs-idiom | Fixed expression describing characteristic |
| "days of old" | figs-metonymy | Noun "days" representing a time period |
| "a foolish person" | No note needed | Adjective modifying explicit noun |
| "he is wise" | No note needed | Adjective as predicate, not noun |

## Key Content Team Decisions (Issues Resolved)

### Aug 20, 2025 - Hebrew adjective "poor" (ani)
> We agreed that in Psalms we would translate the adjective ani as "poor." (If we said "afflicted," that would suggest this was a passive verbal form, but it isn't.) It is often used as a nominal adjective, so we would translate it as "the poor" and write a figs-nominaladj note with an AT such as [poor people]. This is similar to how our resources treat terms such as "the righteous" and "the wicked" in Proverbs. (In the case of the verb form anah, we would translate a passive participle as "afflicted ones.")

### Apr 16, 2025 - Participles as adjectives vs. nouns
> When the translation issue is a nominal adjective (figs-nominaladj), when a participle is being used as an adjective, we would not add the word "one(s)," e.g. Deuteronomy 16:19, "a bribe blinds the eyes of the wise," not "the wise ones." But when a participle is being used as a noun, we will add the word "one(s)," e.g. 1 Samuel 2:30, "For the ones honoring me, I will honor."

### Mar 11, 2026 - Participles used as nouns do not get figs-nominaladj notes
> We do not write figs-nominaladj notes to participles that are used as nouns. These appear in the ULT either in the English -er form (e.g. Psalm 94:4, "doers") or in the noun phrase "the one(s) —ing" (e.g. Psalm 103:18, "the ones keeping"). We do write figs-nominaladj notes to participles used as adjectives, to which the ULT does not add "ones." These are often passive participles (e.g. 2 Samuel 6:1, "the chosen").

### Dec 10, 2025 - "days of old" is NOT figs-nominaladj
> Since "old" is an adjective, having the phrase "in days of old" or "from days of old" in the ULT involves using a nominalized adjective when the Hebrew actually has a noun. We agreed to use a noun to represent kedem in the ULT and say "in/from days of antiquity."

| ULT Expression | Meaning | Example AT |

### 5. Participial Adjectives
Participles used adjectivally that function as nouns:

| Pattern | Example | Treatment |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Participle as adjective | "the wise" (sapiential) | Use base adjective, no "one(s)" |
| Participle as noun | "the ones honoring me" | Use "one(s)" form |
| Passive participle | "the slain" | [people who were killed] |

### 6. Religious/Political Titles
Adjectives used as titles:

| ULT Expression | Meaning | Example AT |
|----------------|---------|------------|
| the anointed of Yahweh | the anointed one of Yahweh | [the one whom Yahweh anointed] |
| the righteous | the righteous one | [the righteous person] |
| the mighty | mighty warriors | [mighty warriors] |
| the uncircumcised | uncircumcised people | [people who are not circumcised] |

**Note:** These are figs-nominaladj, not translate-unknown (the title meaning is clear from context).

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify the article + adjective pattern**: Look for "the [adjective]" without an explicit noun
   - "the righteous" (no noun) -> LIKELY figs-nominaladj
   - "the righteous man" (has noun) -> NOT figs-nominaladj

2. **Check if it represents a class**: Does the adjective refer to ALL people/things with that quality?
   - "The righteous will inherit the land" (= all righteous people) -> figs-nominaladj
   - "The righteous king" (specific person) -> NOT figs-nominaladj

3. **Check for quantity adjectives**: Numbers and quantifiers often function nominally
   - "many followed him" (many = many people) -> figs-nominaladj
   - "all have sinned" (all = all people) -> figs-nominaladj

4. **Check context for what is meant**:
   - Is it people? -> [adjective + people/person]
   - Is it things? -> [adjective + things]
   - Is it one of two? -> [the other + noun from context]

## Common Patterns in Published Notes

### Standard Note Format
"SPEAKER is using the adjective **WORD** as a noun to mean a certain kind of person. Your language may use adjectives in the same way. If not, you can translate this adjective with an equivalent phrase. Alternate translation: [text]"

### Multiple Nominal Adjectives
"SPEAKER is using the adjectives **WORD** and **WORD** as nouns to mean certain kinds of people. Your language may use adjectives in the same way. If not, you can translate these adjectives with equivalent phrases. Alternate translation: [text]"

### Cross-Reference Pattern
"See how you translated **a wicked {one}** in [9:7](../09/07.md)."

## Examples from Published Notes

### Proverbs (Character Types)
- "**the righteous**" -> "righteous people" (11:9)
- "**a wicked {one}**" -> "a wicked person" (9:7)
- "**the upright**" -> "upright people" (11:6)
- "**the stupid**" -> "stupid people" (14:8)
- "**the proud**" -> "proud people" (15:25)
- "**the lazy**" -> "lazy ones" (15:19)

### Matthew (Beatitudes and Beyond)
- "**the poor**" -> "people who are poor" (5:3)
- "**the meek**" -> "meek people" (5:5)
- "**the merciful**" -> "merciful people" (5:7)
- "**the pure**" -> "people who are pure" (5:8)
- "**the blind**" -> "blind people" (11:5)
- "**the dead**" -> "dead people" (8:22)

### Joshua (Age Groups)
- "from **young** and to **old**" -> "from young people to old people" (6:21)
- "with his **firstborn** ... and with his **youngest**" -> "with his oldest son ... with his youngest son" (6:26)

### Romans (Groups)
- "**the ungodly**" -> "people who are ungodly" (4:5)
- "**the many**" -> "many people" (5:15)
- "**all**" -> "all people" (3:23, 5:12)

# writing-politeness

## Purpose
Identify polite language, honorific titles, or courteous expressions that may need translation guidance.

## Definition
**Politeness** refers to language used to communicate respect, deference, humility, or courtesy toward the person being addressed. Every language has ways to communicate politeness, but different languages do not communicate politeness in the same ways. A phrase that is polite in one language may be offensive or confusing in another.

Key characteristics:
- People say things in certain ways to show respect
- Polite forms may be used to avoid offending others
- Especially important when addressing powerful or influential persons
- Includes both verbal phrases and forms of address

---

## When to Use writing-politeness

Use this issue when:
1. **Respectful titles** are used (my lord, my master, sir)
2. **Humble self-reference** is used (your servant, your female servant, your handmaid)
3. **"Behold me"** expresses availability to a superior
4. **Polite understatement** minimizes hospitality offers ("a little water," "a piece of bread")
5. **Courteous greetings** address someone of status ("O king, live forever!")
6. **Affectionate address** from superior to inferior ("my son," "my daughter")
7. **Polite requests** need to not sound rude or demanding

## Confirmed Classifications from Issues Resolved

| Pattern | Decision | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| "Behold me" (availability to superior) | writing-politeness | Courteous expression showing readiness to obey |
| "my lord" as respectful title | writing-politeness | Not actual lordship, shows respect |
| "your servant" (self-reference) | writing-politeness | Speaker is not actually a servant |
| Polite understatement in hospitality | writing-politeness | "a piece of bread" = generous meal |

### Special Case: "Behold me" (hineni)

The expression "behold me" has different interpretations based on context:

| Context | Issue Type | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Inferior expressing availability to superior | **writing-politeness** | Samuel responding to Eli (1 Sam. 3:4) |
| Yahweh announcing action to punish/bless | **figs-idiom** | "Behold me rebuking" = "Now I am about to rebuke" |
| Speaker calling attention to what follows | **figs-metaphor** | "Behold" = "Listen" or "Pay attention" |

### Special Case: "my lord" (adoni)

| Context | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| Addressing someone of higher status | Respectful title, not actual lordship |
| Wife addressing husband (e.g., Sarah) | Title of respect for husband |
| Addressing God | Use figs-youformal if language has formal "you" |

## Common Patterns Requiring writing-politeness Notes

### 1. Respectful Titles

| Expression | Example | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "my lord" (adoni) | Ruth 2:13, Judges 6:13, 1 Sam. 1:15 | Addressing superior (Boaz, angel, priest) |
| "my master" | Various | Addressing authority figure |
| "sir" | Various | Polite address |
| "O king, live forever!" | Royal addresses | Courteous greeting to royalty |

### 2. Humble Self-Reference

| Expression | Example | Context |
|------------|---------|---------|
| "your servant" | Gen. 32:4-5 | Jacob to Esau - showing deference |
| "your female servant" | Ruth 2:13, 3:9 | Ruth to Boaz - not actual servant |
| "your handmaid" | 1 Sam. 25:41 | Abigail expressing willingness to serve |

### 3. "Behold me" (Availability Expression)

| Example | Context |
|---------|---------|
| Gen. 22:1 | Abraham responding to God |
| 1 Sam. 3:4 | Samuel responding to Eli |
| 1 Sam. 14:7 | Armor-bearer to Jonathan |

**Template**: "SPEAKER is responding with the courteous expression that a person in this culture would use to tell a superior that he had heard a summons and was ready and willing to obey. Your language may have a comparable expression that you can use in your translation. Alternate translation: [Yes, sir?] or [At your service]"

### 4. Polite Understatement in Hospitality

| Expression | Example | Actual Meaning |
|------------|---------|----------------|
| "a little water" | Gen. 18:4-5 | Generous water supply |
| "a piece/bit of bread" | Gen. 18:4-5, 1 Kings 17:11 | Full meal |

### 5. Affectionate/Courteous Address

| Expression | Example | Relationship |
|------------|---------|--------------|
| "my son" | 1 Sam. 3:6, 16; Joshua 7:19 | Elder to younger (Eli to Samuel, Joshua to Achan) |
| "my daughter" | Ruth 2:2, 3:1 | Elder to younger woman (Naomi to Ruth) |

### 6. Polite Requests

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Command too direct | Gen. 23:4 "Give to me" - should sound polite |
| "No" too direct | Gen. 23:11 "No, my lord" - soften in some cultures |
| Suggestion to peers | Gen. 29:7 - may need question form |

## NOT writing-politeness (Use These Instead)

### Use figs-123person for:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Speaker referring to self in third person | About person shift, not politeness |
| Addressing someone in third person | May overlap but 123person primary |
| "your servant" in Psalms/prayers (self-reference) | Person shift is the translation concern |

**Note on overlap**: figs-123person and writing-politeness can overlap for "your servant." In Psalms and prayers where the psalmist addresses God as "your servant" meaning himself, use **figs-123person** -- the translation concern is the third-person self-reference, and published TNs consistently classify it that way. Reserve writing-politeness for narrative contexts where the social dynamics of the polite expression are the main concern (e.g., Jacob to Esau, Ruth to Boaz).

### Use figs-idiom for:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| "Behold me" when Yahweh announces action | Means "Now I am about to..." |
| "in the eyes of" | Idiomatic meaning |
| "I beg of you" | Idiomatic polite request |

### Use figs-metaphor for:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| "behold" calling attention to what follows | "Look" means "listen" |
| "brothers" for fellow Israelites | Metaphorical use |

### Use figs-youformal for:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Languages with formal "you" forms | About pronoun forms, not phrases |
| Addressing God formally | Pronoun choice issue |

### Use translate-symaction for:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Kneeling/bowing before someone | Physical action of respect |
| Falling down before someone | Same |

### Use figs-euphemism for:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Polite reference to unpleasant topics | Death, bodily functions |
| "He fell asleep" for death | Euphemism, not politeness |

### Use figs-imperative for:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Imperative used as polite request | Grammatical issue |
| "You pray to the Lord for me" | About imperative form |

### Use figs-rquestion for:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Question form making complaint polite | About rhetorical question |
| "Lord, are you not concerned...?" | Polite complaint via question |

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify polite language in the text**:
   - Look for titles of address (my lord, sir, master, O king)
   - Look for humble self-reference (your servant, your handmaid)
   - Look for "behold me" / "Here I am" expressions
   - Look for understated hospitality offers
   - Check if requests could sound rude

2. **Determine the context**:

   | Relationship | Typical Patterns |
   |--------------|------------------|
   | Inferior to superior | "my lord," "your servant," "behold me" |
   | Host to guest | Understated hospitality |
   | Elder to younger | "my son," "my daughter" |
   | Subject to ruler | "O king, live forever!" |
   | Any respectful address | Titles and courteous phrases |

3. **Check for overlapping issues**:

   | Overlap | Resolution |
   |---------|------------|
   | figs-123person + writing-politeness | Use writing-politeness if politeness is main concern |
   | figs-idiom + writing-politeness | Check Issues Resolved for "behold me" patterns |
   | figs-imperative + writing-politeness | Use figs-imperative for grammatical issue |

4. **Decide on note content**:

   | Pattern | Note Focus |
   |---------|------------|
   | Respectful title | Comparable title in target language |
   | Humble self-reference | Explain not actual servant/slave |
   | "Behold me" | Courteous availability expression |
   | Understatement | Explain polite minimizing |

## Honorifics (Special Category)

Honorific language addresses important, powerful, or honored persons:

| Type | Example | Note Approach |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Titles | "most excellent Governor Felix" | Official honorific title |
| Greeting formulas | "O king, live forever!" | Respectful greeting to royalty |
| Royal references | "King Abimelech" | May need honorific in target language |

Some languages require honorific titles every time a person of high status is mentioned by name.

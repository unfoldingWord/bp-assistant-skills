# figs-exclamations

## Purpose
Identify exclamations - words or sentences that show strong feeling such as surprise, joy, fear, anger, or amazement.

## Definition
An exclamation is an utterance that expresses strong emotion:
- **Exclamation words** - "Oh," "Alas," "Behold," "Hush," "How"
- **Exclamation sentences** - "What manner of stones!" "How unsearchable are his judgments!"
- **Verbless exclamations** - "You worthless person!"
- **Attention-getters** - "Behold," "See," "Listen"

---

## Special Case: Hebrew "Ashrei" (Happiness)

Per Issues Resolved: "The happiness of..." is flagged as figs-exclamations when:
- Opens a psalm or wisdom saying
- "The happiness of the one who..." pattern
- Context suggests praise or blessing

## NOT figs-exclamations

### Use figs-rquestion for:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| "Who can stand before him?" | Question form for rhetorical effect |
| "What is man that you are mindful of him?" | Question expressing wonder |

### "Behold" classifications (per Issues Resolved):
| Context | Use |
|---------|-----|
| "Behold" calling attention to what speaker says | figs-metaphor (look = listen) |
| "Behold me" (inferior to superior) | writing-politeness |
| "Behold me" (Yahweh announcing) | figs-idiom (= "Now I am about to...") |
| "Behold" with exclamation emphasis | figs-exclamations |

## Categories

### 1. "How" Exclamations (Amazement/Emphasis)
**Markers:** "How" + adjective/verb, exclamation point, context shows amazement

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "How unsearchable are his judgments!" | Rom 11:33 |
| "How beautiful are your feet!" | Song 7:1 |
| "How I hated correction!" | Prov 5:12 |
| "How good is a word in its time!" | Prov 15:23 |

### 2. "Alas/Oh/Ah" Exclamations (Distress/Strong Feeling)
**Markers:** "Alas," "Oh," "Ah," context shows distress/fear/sadness

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "Alas, O my Lord Yahweh!" | Judges 6:22 |
| "Ah, Lord Yahweh!" | Neh 1:5, 1:11 |
| "Oh, the depth of the riches!" | Rom 11:33 |

### 3. "Behold" as Attention-Getter
**Markers:** "Behold" or "See" at start, focuses audience, may express surprise

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "Behold, I am sending my messenger" | Mark 1:2 |
| "behold, Barak was pursuing Sisera" | Judges 4:22 |
| "And behold" | Ruth 3:8 |

### 4. Verbless Exclamations
**Markers:** No main verb, strong emotional content, often insults/praise

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "You worthless person!" | Matt 5:22 |
| "Audacious ones! Self-willed ones!" | 2 Pet 2:10 |

### 5. "What" Exclamations (Amazement at Size/Quality)
**Markers:** "What manner of..." expressing amazement, not seeking information

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "What manner of stones and what manner of buildings!" | Mark 13:1 |

### 6. Urgent Commands ("Hush!")
**Markers:** Single word commanding silence or attention, urgent

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "Hush from the face of Lord Yahweh!" | Zeph 1:7 |

### 7. Mock Expressions
**Markers:** Context shows mockery/sarcasm

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "Aha!" | Mark 15:29 |

### 8. Direct Address ("O")
**Markers:** "O" before name/title, strong feeling, often prayer/formal speech

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "O man" | Rom 2:1 |
| "O God" | Heb 1:8 |
| "O Lord" | Heb 1:10 |

---

## Recognition Process

1. **Look for exclamation markers**: Oh, Alas, Ah, Behold, Hush, What, How; exclamation marks; verbless sentences with strong emotion
2. **Identify the emotion**: Fear/distress (Alas), Amazement (How, What), Joy (ashrei), Urgency (Hush), Mockery (Aha)
3. **Check for special classifications**: "Behold" may be figs-metaphor, writing-politeness, or figs-idiom

---

## Key Distinctions

| Expression | Use |
|------------|-----|
| "How unsearchable!" (exclamation) | figs-exclamations |
| "Who can understand?" (question for emphasis) | figs-rquestion |
| "Behold" calling attention (look = listen) | figs-metaphor |
| "Behold" with genuine surprise/emphasis | figs-exclamations |
| "Behold me" (servant responding) | writing-politeness |

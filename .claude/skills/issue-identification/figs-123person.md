# figs-123person

## Purpose
Identify instances where a speaker refers to himself or the person being addressed using a different grammatical person than expected.

## Definition
A speaker normally refers to himself as "I" (first person) and to the person he is speaking to as "you" (second person). This translation issue occurs when:
- A speaker uses third person to refer to **himself** (e.g., "your servant" instead of "I")
- A speaker uses third person to refer to the **addressee** (e.g., "the king" instead of "you")
- God/Yahweh refers to **himself** in the third person (e.g., "Yahweh" instead of "I")

---

## CONFIRMED Classifications from Issues Resolved

**Key Decision (Sep 3, 2025)**:
> "There are two types of statements introduced by 'Thus says Yahweh.' In one type, Yahweh gives the prophet a message to speak about himself (in the third person). In the other type, Yahweh gives the prophet a message to speak on his behalf (in the first person). We would NOT write figs-123person notes for the first type. However, if first person and third person are MIXED within the message, then we would write figs-123person notes for the instances of the third person."

---

## NOT figs-123person (Do NOT Use This Issue)

| Situation | Why NOT | Use Instead |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Prophetic message entirely in 3rd person about Yahweh | Prophet speaking ABOUT God, not FOR God | No note needed |
| Generic/hypothetical "a man who does X" | Standard 3rd person reference | No note needed |
| Indefinite "they" meaning people in general | Different issue | writing-pronouns |
| Narrative descriptions of characters | Standard storytelling | No note needed |
| Titles used as regular nouns | Not a person shift | translate-names if needed |

## Categories/Subtypes

### 1. Self-Reference for Humility (Most Common)
Speaker refers to himself in third person to show humility before a superior.

**Common phrases**: "your servant," "your female servant," "your maidservant"

**Examples**:
- Hannah: "your female servant...your female servant...give to your female servant" (1 Sam 1:11)
- David to Saul: "Your servant used to keep his father's sheep" (1 Sam 17:34)
- Samuel: "for your servant is listening" (1 Sam 3:9)
- Simeon: "You are now dismissing your servant" (Luke 2:29)
- Moses: "your servant" (Exod 4:10)

### 2. Addressee-Reference for Respect
Speaker refers to the person being addressed in third person to show respect.

**Common phrases**: "my Lord," "the king," "if it is good to the king"

**Examples**:
- Abraham to Yahweh: "Please do not let my Lord be angry" (Gen 18:30)
- Esther to the king: "If it is good to the king" (Esth 5:4)
- David to Saul: "to the king" instead of "to you" (1 Sam 18:18)
- Jonathan to Saul: "May the king not sin against his servant" (1 Sam 19:4)
- Memukan to Ahasuerus: "the king Ahasuerus" (Esth 1:16-20)

### 3. Divine Self-Reference (Yahweh/God in 3rd Person)
Yahweh or God speaks about himself in third person, often for emphasis or majesty.

**Examples**:
- God to Job: "Do you have an arm like God's?" (Job 40:9)
- God about the covenant: "between God and..." (Gen 9:16)
- Yahweh: "the hand of Yahweh" (Exod 9:3)
- Yahweh: "the day of Yahweh" (Zeph 1:14, Mal 4:5)
- Yahweh: "to listen to the voice of Yahweh your God" (Exod 15:26)

### 4. Messianic Self-Reference ("Son of Man" / "the Son")
Jesus refers to himself in third person using titles, especially "the Son of Man."

**Very common in**: John, Luke, Matthew, Mark

**Examples**:
- Jesus: "the Son of Man" (John 1:51, 3:13-14, 5:19-27, Luke 5:24, 9:22)
- Jesus: "the Son" (John 3:17, 5:19-23)
- Jesus: "the light" (John 3:19-21)
- Jesus: "the one having descended from heaven" (John 3:13)
- Jesus: "something greater than Solomon/Jonah is here" (Luke 11:31-32)

### 5. Epistolary Convention
Letter writers refer to themselves in third person when introducing themselves.

**Examples**:
- Paul: "Paul...to the church" (1 Cor 1:1)
- Paul: "The greeting of Paul" (1 Cor 16:21)
- John: "to his servant John" (Rev 1:1)
- John: "John to the seven churches" (Rev 1:4)

### 6. Mixed Person in Same Speech
Shifts between grammatical persons referring to the same entity within a single speech or passage. This includes any mid-verse or mid-passage shift — not just self-reference.

**Examples**:
- Jacob's vow: "If God will be with me...then Yahweh will be God to me" (Gen 28:20-21)
- Yahweh mixing "I" and "he/Yahweh" in prophecy
- Jesus mixing "I" and "the Son" in discourse
- Author shifting from 3rd person ("his neighbor") to 2nd person ("your wineskin") when addressing the same person (Hab 2:15)

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify the speaker and addressee**: Who is speaking to whom?

2. **Check for unexpected person reference**:
   - Does the speaker use "your servant" or similar when referring to self? -> Type 1
   - Does the speaker use titles/3rd person for the addressee? -> Type 2
   - Does God/Yahweh speak of himself as "God/Yahweh/he"? -> Type 3
   - Does Jesus use "Son of Man/the Son"? -> Type 4
   - Is this a letter introduction? -> Type 5
   - Is there mixing of persons for same referent? -> Type 6

3. **Check Issues Resolved exclusions**:
   - Is this a prophetic message ENTIRELY in 3rd person? -> NO note needed
   - Is this generic/hypothetical language? -> NO note needed

4. **Determine the purpose**:
   - Humility (speaker lowering self)
   - Respect (speaker honoring addressee)
   - Emphasis/Majesty (divine self-reference)
   - Christological title (Messianic claims)
   - Convention (epistolary form)

## Common Triggering Vocabulary

### Hebrew
- **eved** (servant) - "servant" (self-reference)
- **amah/shifchah** - "female servant/maidservant" (self-reference)
- **adoni** - "my lord" (addressee reference)
- **hammelekh** - "the king" (addressee reference)
- Yahweh/God names in divine speech

### Greek
- **ho doulos sou** - "your servant" (self-reference)
- **ho huios tou anthropou** - "the Son of Man" (Jesus self-reference)
- **ho huios** - "the Son" (Jesus self-reference)
- Author names in letter openings

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Humility (1 Samuel 17:34)
**Text**: "Your servant used to keep his father's sheep"
**Speaker**: David
**Addressee**: Saul
**Analysis**: David uses "your servant" and "his" instead of "I" and "my"
**Type**: Self-reference for humility
**Template**: figs-123person,self

### Example 2: Respect (Genesis 18:30)
**Text**: "Please do not let my Lord be angry"
**Speaker**: Abraham
**Addressee**: Yahweh
**Analysis**: Abraham speaks OF Yahweh as "my Lord" instead of TO Yahweh as "you"
**Type**: Addressee-reference for respect
**Template**: figs-123person,addressee

### Example 3: Divine Self-Reference (Job 40:9)
**Text**: "Do you have an arm like God's?"
**Speaker**: Yahweh
**Addressee**: Job
**Analysis**: Yahweh says "God's" instead of "mine"
**Type**: Divine self-reference for emphasis
**Template**: figs-123person,Yahweh

### Example 4: Messianic Self-Reference (John 3:14)
**Text**: "the Son of Man to be lifted up"
**Speaker**: Jesus
**Addressee**: Nicodemus
**Analysis**: Jesus refers to himself as "the Son of Man" instead of "I"
**Type**: Messianic self-reference
**Note**: Special adaptation needed - not standard humility/respect

### Example 5: NOT figs-123person (Prophetic Message)
**Text**: "Thus says Yahweh: Yahweh will punish..." (entire message in 3rd person)
**Analysis**: Prophet delivering message ABOUT Yahweh, not speaking AS Yahweh
**Decision**: NO figs-123person note per Issues Resolved

## Quality Checklist

Before applying this classification, verify:
- [ ] Speaker and addressee are correctly identified
- [ ] The reference is genuinely unexpected (not standard 3rd person)
- [ ] This is NOT a prophetic message entirely in 3rd person
- [ ] The purpose (humility/respect/emphasis) is clear
- [ ] The appropriate template subtype is selected

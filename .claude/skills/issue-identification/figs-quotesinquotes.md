# figs-quotesinquotes

## Purpose
Identify places where nested quotations (quotes inside quotes) may create difficulty for translators.

## Definition
A **quote within a quote** occurs when a speaker quotes someone else's words within their own speech. Each additional level of quotation creates a new "layer":

- **Level 1**: The outer quotation (what the current speaker says)
- **Level 2**: A quotation inside level 1 (what someone else said)
- **Level 3**: A quotation inside level 2 (what a third party said)
- **Level 4**: And so on...

Key characteristics:
- Multiple layers can confuse readers about who is speaking
- Pronouns become ambiguous across layers
- Some languages struggle with more than 2-3 levels of direct quotation
- Converting inner levels to indirect quotes can simplify the structure

---

## When to Use figs-quotesinquotes

Use this issue when:
1. There are **two or more levels of direct quotation** nested together
2. The nesting creates potential **pronoun confusion**
3. Translators might benefit from **converting an inner quote to indirect speech**
4. Languages with different quotation conventions need guidance

### Quotation Levels in Biblical Text

| Levels | Example Pattern | Difficulty |
|--------|-----------------|------------|
| 2 levels | Jesus said, "They will say, 'I am the Christ'" | Moderate |
| 3 levels | He said, "I told her, 'Say, "He is my brother"'" | High |
| 4 levels | They said, "A man said, 'Tell him, "Yahweh says, '...'"'" | Very High |

## Common Patterns in Biblical Text

### Two-Level Quotations (Most Common)

1. **Scripture citations**: "It is written, 'You shall not...'"
2. **Reported dialogue**: "He asked, 'Who said, "I am the Christ"?'"
3. **Instructions to speak**: "Go and say to him, 'The king commands...'"
4. **Remembered speech**: "I said to her, 'You must tell them...'"

### Three-Level Quotations

1. **Prophetic commissioning**: "Go to the king and say, 'Yahweh says, "...'"
2. **Narrative flashbacks**: "Abraham said, 'I told her, "Say about me, 'He is my brother'"'"
3. **Reported prophetic messages**: Messenger reporting what prophet said God said

### Four-Level Quotations (Rare but Challenging)

- 2 Kings 1:6 is the classic example: Messengers telling the king what a man told them to tell the king that Yahweh said

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify the speaker layers**:
   - Who is the outer speaker (level 1)?
   - Who is being quoted at level 2?
   - Are there additional levels?

2. **Check for pronoun ambiguity**:
   - Could "I" or "you" be misunderstood across levels?
   - Are the referents clear at each level?

3. **Assess complexity**:
   - How many levels of quotation?
   - How long is each quoted section?
   - Are quote boundaries clearly marked?

4. **Decide on strategy**:

   | Complexity | Strategy |
   |------------|----------|
   | 2 levels, clear | May not need a note |
   | 2 levels, long inner quote | Note about keeping direct |
   | 3+ levels | Usually suggest converting some to indirect |
   | Pronoun confusion | Always write a note |

---

## Strategies for Handling Nested Quotes

The Translation Academy suggests these strategies:

### Strategy 1: Keep All as Direct Quotes
- Use alternating quotation mark styles (double, single, double...)
- Best when the quoted material is central to the meaning
- Example: Scripture citations where the exact words matter

### Strategy 2: Convert Inner Quotes to Indirect
- Reduces complexity by eliminating deeper levels
- Changes pronouns to narrator's perspective
- Example: "He told them that a man had said to go back..."

### Strategy 3: Combination Approach
- Keep most important quotes direct
- Convert less critical ones to indirect
- Often the best solution for 3+ levels

## Critical Distinctions

### Scripture Citations
When NT writers quote OT Scripture within their speech:
- The quotation itself should usually remain direct (the exact words matter)
- Write notes to help translators mark the boundaries clearly
- Often works with figs-quotemarks note as well

### Prophetic Messages
When prophets quote Yahweh:
- Usually keep Yahweh's words as direct quotation
- May convert the surrounding narrative to indirect
- Example: "Yahweh told me to tell you, 'I will...'" vs "Yahweh commanded me to tell you that he will..."

### Commands to Speak
When someone is told what to say:
- The content to be spoken often needs to stay direct
- The outer frame can become indirect if helpful
- Example: "Tell him, 'The king commands...'" may be more natural than indirect version

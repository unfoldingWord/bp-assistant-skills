# translate-symaction

## Purpose
Identify physical actions that carry cultural or religious meaning beyond their literal performance.

## Definition

**Symbolic action** is something a person does to express a certain idea. The action itself has meaning in the culture - it communicates respect, distress, worship, dedication, or other concepts. The reader may need to understand the cultural significance to grasp the full meaning.

Key aspects:
- A PERSON performs an ACTION
- The action carries cultural or religious MEANING
- The meaning may not be obvious to readers from other cultures
- Actions may be intentional (bowing to show respect) or may carry symbolic meaning even when accidental (Dagon falling prostrate = submission)

## Authoritative Decision

From Issues Resolved (May 14, 2025):
> We agreed to use translate-symaction for actions that are symbolic that God introduces as well as for symbolic actions that the culture already practiced.

This means symaction applies to:
- Cultural practices (bowing, tearing clothes)
- God-instituted symbolic acts (laying hands on sacrifice, circumcision)

## Distinction from Related Issues

### NOT writing-symlanguage (Symbolic Language)
- **symaction**: A PERSON DOES something with symbolic meaning
- **symlanguage**: A TEXT DESCRIBES something symbolic (visions, dreams, apocalyptic imagery)

Examples:
- "fell at Jesus' feet" = **symaction** (person's action shows respect)
- "the seven lampstands are the seven churches" = **symlanguage** (vision image represents churches)
- "tore his robe and shaved his head" = **symaction** (person shows distress)
- "a fourth animal with iron teeth" = **symlanguage** (vision image represents kingdom)

### NOT figs-metaphor
- **symaction**: Literal action with symbolic meaning
- **metaphor**: Figurative comparison (A spoken of AS IF B)

Example:
- "sitting on a throne" when describing actual ruling = **symaction**
- "you are the salt of the earth" = **metaphor**

## Recognition Process

```
Does the text describe a person performing an action?
  |
  +-- Is the action a culturally meaningful gesture?
  |     |
  |     Examples: bowing, prostrating, tearing clothes, raising hands
  |     |
  |     YES --> translate-symaction
  |
  +-- Is the action a God-instituted ritual act?
  |     |
  |     Examples: laying hands on sacrifice, pouring oil, circumcision
  |     |
  |     YES --> translate-symaction
  |
  +-- Does the action communicate meaning beyond the physical act?
  |     |
  |     Examples: sitting on throne (= ruling), wearing ephod (= priestly service)
  |     |
  |     YES --> translate-symaction
  |
  +-- Is it just a descriptive image in a vision/dream?
        |
        NO action by a person --> writing-symlanguage (not symaction)
```

## Common Categories

### 1. Gestures of Respect/Worship
- **Bowing/prostrating**: "fell on his face," "bowed down," "prostrated himself"
- **Falling at feet**: Shows submission, honor, or desperate plea
- Meaning: Deep respect, worship, submission to authority

### 2. Gestures of Distress/Mourning
- **Tearing clothes**: "tore his robe"
- **Shaving head**: "shaved his head"
- **Sitting on ground**: "sat with him on the ground"
- Meaning: Deep grief, distress, sympathy

### 3. Ritual/Religious Acts
- **Laying hands on animal**: Identification with sacrifice
- **Pouring oil**: Dedication to God ("anointed a pillar")
- **Sprinkling blood**: Atonement, purification
- **Not cutting hair**: Nazirite vow dedication

### 4. Status/Authority Actions
- **Sitting on throne**: Indicates ruling/authority
- **Wearing special garments**: Ephod = priestly service
- **Placing hand under thigh**: Oath-taking gesture

### 5. Cultural Customs
- **Covering with veil**: Modesty, submission (bride)
- **Standing at door and knocking**: Requesting welcome
- **Palm branches**: Victory celebration

### 6. Actions with Implied Meaning
- **Eating after distress**: Hannah ate = no longer distressed
- **Statue falling prostrate**: Dagon = submission (even if accidental)

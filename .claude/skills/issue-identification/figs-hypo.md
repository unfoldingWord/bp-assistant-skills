# figs-hypo

## Purpose
Identify hypothetical (imaginary/unreal) situations in biblical text where speakers use imaginary scenarios to teach, illustrate, express regret, or make a point.

## Definition
A hypothetical situation is an IMAGINARY situation - something that is not real. It may be:
- In the past (did not happen)
- In the present (is not happening)
- In the future (is not expected to happen)

Key characteristic: The situation is UNREAL or CONTRARY-TO-FACT.

---

## CRITICAL DISTINCTION: figs-hypo vs grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical

These two issues are easily confused. The distinction is based on the Issues Resolved document (Aug 6, 2025):

| Issue | Use For | Key Test |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **figs-hypo** | IMAGINARY conditions ("if I were you") | Is the situation UNREAL/impossible/contrary-to-fact? |
| **grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical** | SIMPLE conditions where "if" or "then" is unstated | Is it a REAL possible condition that needs grammatical clarification? |

### figs-hypo: IMAGINARY Situations
- Contrary-to-fact past: "If only we had died" (we didn't die)
- Counterfactual present: "If he were here" (he is not here)
- Absurd/impossible scenarios: body parts talking to each other
- Imaginary scenarios for teaching: "Suppose someone said to God..."
- Wishes/regrets: "I wish he had come"

### grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical: REAL Possible Conditions
- Legal case law: "A man, when he has..." (= "If someone has...")
- Missing "if": "Do this, and you will live" (= "If you do this...")
- Missing "then": consequence not clearly marked
- Reordering needed: "then" comes before "if"

**Quick Test**:
- Could this actually happen or is it real possibility? --> grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical
- Is this imaginary, impossible, or contrary to what actually happened? --> figs-hypo

## Categories of figs-hypo

### 1. Contrary-to-Fact Past (Regrets/Wishes)
Things that did NOT happen but the speaker imagines what would have been.

**Markers**: "If only...", "Would that...", "I wish..."

**Examples**:
- Exodus 16:3: "If only we had died" - They did NOT die; expressing wish/regret
- Genesis 31:27: "I would have sent you with joy" - Laban did NOT know Jacob was leaving
- Genesis 31:42: "If God had not been with me" - God WAS with Jacob (contrary-to-fact)
- Job 3:13: "If I had died at birth, then I would be at rest" - Job did NOT die at birth
- Matthew 11:21: "If the mighty deeds had been done in Tyre and Sidon" - They were NOT done there

### 2. Contrary-to-Fact Present
Things that are NOT true right now but the speaker imagines as if they were.

**Examples**:
- Luke 5:37: "But if he did put new wine into old wineskins" - No one actually does this
- John 11:21: "Lord, if you had been here, my brother would not have died" - Jesus was NOT there

### 3. Absurd/Impossible Imaginary Scenarios
Used to illustrate a point through impossible situations.

**Examples**:
- 1 Corinthians 12:15-21: Body parts talking ("If the foot would say...") - Feet cannot talk; absurd scenario to teach about church unity
- Matthew 12:11: "If a man had just one sheep and it fell into a hole" - Imaginary scenario to make a point

### 4. Future Unlikely/Not Expected
Things that probably will NOT happen but the speaker imagines what would occur if they did.

**Examples**:
- Matthew 24:22: "Unless those days are shortened, no flesh would be saved" - Days WILL be shortened
- Galatians 5:11: "If I still proclaim circumcision, why am I still being persecuted?" - Paul does NOT proclaim circumcision

### 5. Imaginary Scenarios for Teaching
The speaker invents a scenario to help explain or challenge.

**Examples**:
- Job 34:31-33: "Suppose someone said to God..." - Elihu creates imaginary scenario
- 1 John 1:6-10: Series of "If we say..." scenarios to teach about sin and confession
- James: Multiple "suppose..." scenarios for teaching

### 6. Conditional Promises/Warnings Based on Unreal Conditions
The condition attached to the promise/warning is hypothetical.

**Examples**:
- Exodus 8:8: "And I will let the people go" - Conditional on frogs going away (which hasn't happened yet)
- Exodus 34:12: "lest you cut a covenant... lest he become a trap" - Warning using hypotheticals

## NOT figs-hypo (Use These Instead)

### Use grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical for:
| Expression | Why NOT figs-hypo |
|------------|-------------------|
| Legal case law ("A man, when he...") | REAL possible condition, needs "if" clarification |
| "Do this, and you will live" | REAL condition, missing "if" |
| "If your brother sins, rebuke him" | REAL possible event that could happen |
| Conditional where "then" is missing | Grammatical clarification needed, not imaginary |

### Use grammar-connect-condition-fact for:
| Expression | Why NOT figs-hypo |
|------------|-------------------|
| "If" meaning "since" | Factual condition, not hypothetical |
| "If you love me, keep my commands" | Could be factual condition |

### Use grammar-connect-condition-contrary for:

**Key distinction**: Both figs-hypo and grammar-connect-condition-contrary involve unreal conditions, but they serve different purposes:

| Feature | figs-hypo | grammar-connect-condition-contrary |
|---------|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| **Purpose** | TEACH/ILLUSTRATE through imagination | PROVE something through logic |
| **Structure** | "Imagine X. What would happen?" | "if A then B; A is false; so not B" |
| **Speaker's goal** | Help audience understand a principle | Demonstrate a logical conclusion |

**Examples of grammar-connect-condition-contrary (NOT figs-hypo):**
- John 8:42: "If God were your Father, you would love me" - LOGICAL PROOF (they don't love, proves God isn't their father)
- 1 Cor 15:13-19: "If there is no resurrection..." - REDUCTIO AD ABSURDUM (showing absurd results proves resurrection is real)
- Matt 12:26: "If Satan casts out Satan, he is divided" - LOGICAL ARGUMENT (Satan isn't divided, so he doesn't cast out Satan)

**Examples of figs-hypo (NOT condition-contrary):**
- 1 Cor 12:15-21: Body parts talking - ABSURD TEACHING SCENARIO (illustrating church unity)
- Matt 7:9-10: "What man, if his son asks for bread..." - IMAGINARY ILLUSTRATION
- Matt 18:12: "If a man has 100 sheep..." - PARABLE SETUP

**Quick Test**:
- Is the speaker PROVING something by showing what logically follows from a false premise? --> grammar-connect-condition-contrary
- Is the speaker TEACHING by creating an imaginary scenario to illustrate? --> figs-hypo

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify the conditional**: Does the text contain a condition (explicit or implicit)?

2. **Check the reality status**:
   - Did this happen / Is this true / Will this happen?
   - OR is it imaginary/contrary-to-fact/impossible?

3. **Apply the key test**:

   | Question | If YES --> |
   |----------|------------|
   | Is it contrary-to-fact (didn't happen, isn't true)? | figs-hypo |
   | Is it absurd/impossible (body parts talking)? | figs-hypo |
   | Is it a wish/regret about something that didn't happen? | figs-hypo |
   | Is it an imaginary scenario for teaching? | figs-hypo |
   | Is it a real possible condition needing grammatical clarification? | grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical |
   | Is "if" or "then" missing/unclear? | grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical |

4. **Check Issues Resolved** for specific decisions.

## Linguistic Markers

### English Markers for Hypothetical
- "If only..."
- "Would that..."
- "I wish..."
- "Suppose..."
- Subjunctive mood ("If he were..." vs "If he is...")
- "would have + past participle" (counterfactual)
- Past tense with present meaning (unreal condition)

### Hebrew Markers
- x?�_xo (lu) - "If only" / "Would that" (wishes/regrets)
- Imperfect with waw-consecutive in unreal conditions

### Greek Markers
- Second-class conditional (ei + indicative... an + optative) - contrary-to-fact
- Third-class conditional can be either hypothetical or real - context determines

## Alternate Translation Patterns

Common ways to express hypothetical situations:
- "Suppose [X happened]. Then [Y would result]"
- "What if [X]? Then [Y]"
- "Imagine that [X]. In that case, [Y]"
- "If [X] were the case (which it is not), then [Y]"
- "We wish that [X had happened]"

---

## Summary: The Key Distinction

**figs-hypo** = IMAGINARY (unreal, contrary-to-fact, impossible, wishes/regrets)
- "If I were you..." (I am not you)
- "If only we had died..." (we didn't die)
- "Suppose a foot could talk..." (feet cannot talk)

**grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical** = REAL POSSIBLE conditions needing grammatical clarity
- "A man, when he has..." (= "If someone has..." - could really happen)
- "Do this, and you will live" (= "If you do this..." - real condition)

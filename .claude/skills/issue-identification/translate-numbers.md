# translate-numbers

## Purpose
Identify when cardinal numbers in biblical text may need translation notes.

## Definition
Numbers in the Bible are expressed as words or numerals. Some languages lack words for large numbers, and translators need guidance on how to represent exact vs. approximate numbers consistently.

## When to Write Notes

### Large Numbers
Write notes when numbers exceed what some languages can easily express:
- Hundreds (100, 200, 500)
- Thousands (1,000, 10,000)
- Compound numbers (137, 430, 600,000)
- Very large numbers (144,000, 200,000,000)

### Approximate vs. Exact Numbers
**From Issues Resolved**: Consider context to decide whether numbers like "10,000" are exact or approximate. "About 3,000" is approximate; "86 years old" is exact.

Approximate number indicators:
- "about," "approximately," or similar qualifiers
- Round numbers in battle/casualty counts
- Symbolic numbers (7, 12, 40, 70)
- Expressions like "thousands of ten thousands" (rhetorical abundance)

### Numbers as Idiom
The number 10,000 sometimes means "very many" rather than a literal count:
- "10,000 men" in military contexts may be approximate or customary
- "Myriads of myriads" = extremely large, uncountable

## Recognition Patterns

1. **Written numbers in ULT**: seven, forty, one hundred thirty-seven
2. **Numeric form in ULT**: 86, 430, 144,000
3. **Large round numbers**: 10,000, 100,000, 1,000,000
4. **Compound constructions**: "600,000 men on foot"

## ULT/UST Convention

ULT and UST use words for 1-10 and numerals for numbers above 10.

## Categories

### Exact Numbers
- Ages: "86 years old"
- Counts: "162 years," "800 years"
- Specific measurements with numbers

### Round/Approximate Numbers
- Battle casualties: "about 3,000 died"
- Large armies: "10,000 men"
- Rhetorical expressions: "thousands of ten thousands"

### Symbolic Numbers
- 7 (completion, perfection)
- 12 (tribes, apostles)
- 40 (testing period)
- 70 (nations, elders)
- 144,000 (12 x 12 x 1000)

## Examples from Published Notes

**Exodus 12:37**: "about 600,000" - approximate large number
**Revelation 5:11**: "myriads of myriads" - Greek idiom for uncountable
**Judges 1:4**: "10,000 men" - may be approximate or customary
**1 Corinthians 10:8**: "23,000 people" - round number (OT says 24,000)

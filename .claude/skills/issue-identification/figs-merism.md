# figs-merism

## Purpose
Identify merism in biblical text, distinguishing it from lists, metonymy, and synecdoche.

## Definition
Merism is a figure of speech in which a person refers to something by speaking of two (or more) extreme parts of it. By referring to the extreme parts, the speaker intends to include also everything in between those parts.

Key characteristics:
- Names two extremes or two major components to represent the whole
- The intended meaning is the totality, not just the parts mentioned
- The parts are representative, not exhaustive
- Works because the audience understands the convention

## NOT figs-merism (Use These Instead)

### Use figs-metonymy for:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| "the sword" = warfare | Association, not extremes representing whole |
| "the crown" = kingship | Association, not extremes |
| "blood" = death/life | Association concept |

### Use figs-synecdoche for:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| "all hands on deck" | Part for whole (hand = person) |
| "give us this day our daily bread" | Part for whole (bread = food) |

### Use listing/enumeration when:
| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| All items are meant literally | Not using extremes to imply totality |
| Items don't represent a spectrum | Not merism pattern |

## Merism Categories with Examples

### 1. Time Merisms
- **"night and day" / "day and night"** = all the time, constantly
  - "working night and day" = working throughout that time with little rest
  - "they worship day and night" = they worship constantly
- **"yesterday and today and forever"** = all of time (past, present, future)
- **"from the first day to the last day"** = throughout the entire period
- **"from morning to evening"** = within a single day, an entire day
- **"from my youth to this day"** = my entire life
- **"from generation to generation"** = for all people at all times

### 2. Location/Space Merisms
- **"heaven and earth"** = all of creation, everything that exists
  - "Lord of heaven and earth" = Lord over everything
- **"from Dan to Beersheba"** = all of Israel (geographic extremes)
- **"from the rising to the setting of the sun"** = everywhere in the world
- **"from east and west" / "from east to west and north to south"** = from all directions, everywhere
- **"the near and the far"** = everywhere, all locations
- **"distant or near"** = wherever that may be
- **"in the city and in the countryside"** = throughout the whole region
- **"the ends of the earth"** = everywhere
- **"the four corners of the earth"** = everywhere in the world
- **"here or there"** = anywhere
- **"this way and that way"** = everywhere, in all directions
- **"in heaven, on earth, and under the earth"** = anywhere in creation

### 3. People Merisms
- **"young and old" / "from young to old"** = everyone of all ages
- **"small and great" / "from the greatest to the least"** = people of all status
- **"man and woman" / "any man or woman"** = all people, everyone
- **"male servants and female servants"** = all servants
- **"sons and daughters"** = all children, all family members
- **"living and dead"** = all people (whether alive or deceased)
- **"Greeks and barbarians"** = all Gentiles
- **"wise and foolish"** = all types of people
- **"one restrained and one set free"** = all male descendants (slaves and free)
- **"sojourner and native-born"** = everyone regardless of origin

### 4. Body Merisms
- **"from head to foot" / "from the sole of his foot to his pate"** = entire body
- **"before and behind"** = the whole body (front and back)
- **"around and underneath"** = the whole body
- **"flesh and bones"** = a physical body
- **"through its length and through its width"** = the entire area

### 5. Creation/Universal Merisms
- **"heaven and earth"** = everything that exists, all of creation
- **"the visible and the invisible"** = everything (whether seen or unseen)
- **"man and beast"** = all creatures, every living thing
- **"beasts and birds, reptiles and marine animals"** = every kind of creature
- **"the sea and the dry land"** = the entire earth
- **"heaven, earth, sea, and springs of water"** = all of creation
- **"oxen and sheep, camels and donkeys"** = all animals owned

### 6. Scripture Merisms
- **"the Law and the Prophets"** = all of Scripture
- **"Moses and the prophets"** = all of God's Word
- **"the law of Moses, the prophets, and the Psalms"** = all the Hebrew Scriptures

### 7. Extent/Completeness Merisms
- **"a great thing or a small thing"** = anything at all
- **"beginning and ending"** = everything, completely
- **"root and branch"** = nothing remaining, complete destruction
- **"right or left"** = in any direction, at all
- **"the first and the last"** = eternal, existing for all time
- **"the alpha and the omega"** = eternal (first and last Greek letters)
- **"statutes and judgments"** = all laws

### 8. Whole-Being Merisms
- **"heart, soul, mind, strength"** = entire being, all of who one is
- **"heart and soul"** = entire being
- **"psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs"** = all types of praise songs

### 9. Paired Conditions Merisms
- **"to die and to live"** = whatever may happen, come what may
- **"to be filled and to be hungry"** = all conditions
- **"to abound and to be in need"** = all circumstances
- **"bread and water"** = food and drink, sustenance

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify potential merism**: Does the text name two (or more) things that seem to represent extremes or major components?

2. **Check for totality intent**: Is the speaker using these parts to mean "everything" or "all"?

3. **Apply the merism test**:
   - Are two extremes named (e.g., young/old, heaven/earth)?
   - OR are two major components named (e.g., day/night, man/woman)?
   - Is the intent to include everything between/within those parts?
   - Would the audience understand this as referring to the whole?

4. **Distinguish from similar figures**:

   | Test | If YES | If NO |
   |------|--------|-------|
   | Two extremes representing the whole? | Likely merism | Check other figures |
   | Part standing for whole (substitutable)? | Synecdoche | Could be merism |
   | Association-based substitution? | Metonymy | Could be merism |
   | Literal list of items? | Not figurative | Could be merism |

5. **Key distinction: merism vs synecdoche**:
   - Merism: Two or more parts (especially extremes) represent the whole
   - Synecdoche: ONE part represents the whole, or whole represents a part

6. **Key distinction: merism vs list**:
   - Merism: Parts named to imply totality (everything in between included)
   - List: Parts named because those specific items are meant

## Common Merism Patterns

### Extremes Pattern (most common)
Two opposite ends of a spectrum:
- young/old (age spectrum)
- great/small (status/importance spectrum)
- east/west, north/south (directional spectrum)
- first/last (temporal spectrum)
- near/far (distance spectrum)
- rising/setting of sun (daily cycle)

### Components Pattern
Two major parts that together make the whole:
- heaven/earth (creation)
- day/night (time)
- man/woman (humanity)
- living/dead (all people)
- Law/Prophets (Scripture)
- city/countryside (region)

### Multiple Components Pattern
More than two parts representing completeness:
- heart, soul, mind, strength (whole person)
- east, west, north, south (all directions)
- heaven, earth, under the earth (everywhere)
- Law, Prophets, Psalms (all Scripture)

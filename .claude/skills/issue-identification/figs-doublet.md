# figs-doublet

## Purpose
Identify doublets (two or more words/phrases with the same meaning used together for emphasis).

## Definition
A doublet is two words or phrases that mean the same thing (or very close to the same thing), used together to emphasize or intensify the idea. They are usually joined with "and." Unlike hendiadys, where one word modifies the other, in a doublet the words are **equal** synonyms.

**Includes repeated words for emphasis**: "Master! Master!" or "Truly, truly" - repetition of the same word with no other words between them.

---

## Critical Distinctions

### Doublet vs Parallelism (KEY DISTINCTION)

From Issues Resolved: "In general, a doublet is two or more words or phrases with the same meaning used together for emphasis, while a parallelism is two or more clauses (= subject + verb; subject may be implied in an imperative) with the same meaning used together for emphasis."

| Doublet | Parallelism |
|---------|-------------|
| Two **words or short phrases** | Two **clauses** (subject + verb) |
| "scattered and dispersed" | "Praise Yahweh, all nations / exalt him, all peoples" |
| "false and deceptive" | "He who watches over Israel / will neither slumber nor sleep" |
| Usually joined by "and" | Often poetic lines |

**The boundary is fuzzy**: Long synonymous phrases may feel more like parallelism. Editorial discretion applies.

### Doublet vs Hendiadys

| Doublet | Hendiadys |
|---------|-----------|
| Words are **equal synonyms** | One word **modifies** the other |
| "blameless and righteous" = very righteous | "kingdom and glory" = glorious kingdom |
| "scattered and dispersed" = spread out | "spirit and power" = powerful spirit |
| Both words express same meaning | One is primary, one describes it |

**Test**: Can you collapse them to one intensified word? -> Doublet. Does one modify the other (adjective-like relationship)? -> Hendiadys.

### Doublet vs Merism

| Doublet | Merism |
|---------|--------|
| Synonyms for **emphasis** | Extremes representing **totality** |
| "false and deceptive" = lying | "young and old" = everyone |
| Words mean the SAME thing | Words name DIFFERENT ends of spectrum |

## Doublet Categories

### 1. Synonymous Adjectives
Two adjectives with same/similar meaning:
- "blameless and righteous" (Job 1:1) = very righteous
- "false and deceptive" (Dan 2:9) = lying
- "pure and upright" (Job 8:6) = truly righteous
- "great and fearsome" (Neh 4:14) = great and glorious
- "honest and good heart" (Luke 8:15) = genuine intentions
- "faithful and true" (Rev 3:14) = entirely faithful
- "weak and useless" (Heb 7:18) = very ineffective

### 2. Synonymous Nouns
Two nouns with same/similar meaning:
- "outcry and words" (Neh 5:6) = complaints
- "favor and kindness" (Est 2:17) = very pleased
- "commandments and statutes" (Luke 1:6) = everything commanded
- "prayers and requests" (Heb 5:7) = many prayers
- "house and labor" (Neh 5:13) = everything owned
- "decree and law" (Est 4:3) = the letter from the king

### 3. Synonymous Verbs
Two verbs with same/similar meaning:
- "sent and called" (Job 1:4) = invited
- "glorifying and praising" (Luke 2:20) = excitedly praising
- "bow down and prostrate" (Est 3:2) = bowing to the ground
- "formed and made" (Job 10:8) = created
- "sympathize and comfort" (Job 2:11) = earnestly sympathize
- "rebuke and discipline" (Rev 3:19) = diligently correct

### 4. Synonymous Phrases
Two phrases with same meaning:
- "brothers, the Jews" / "our brothers, the Jews" (Neh 5:1, 8) = fellow Jews
- "great power and strong hand" (Neh 1:10) = very great strength
- "the nations, our enemies" (Neh 5:9) = our enemies
- "darkness and shadow of death" (Luke 1:79) = deep spiritual darkness
- "fellow servants and brothers" (Rev 6:11) = all believers

### 5. Word Repetition
Same word repeated for emphasis:
- "Truly, truly" (John 1:51, etc.) = What follows is very true
- "Master! Master!" (Luke 8:24) = urgent calling
- "Yes, Amen" (Rev 1:7) = It will certainly be so
- "rejoices with joy" (John 3:29) = rejoices greatly

### 6. Multiple Synonyms (Extended Doublet)
More than two words with same meaning:
- "annihilate, slaughter, destroy" (Est 3:13) = completely destroy
- "nation, tribe, people, language" (Rev 7:9) = every different people group
- "hour, day, month, year" (Rev 9:15) = that specific time

## Recognition Process

1. **Look for words/phrases joined by "and"** (or repeated consecutively)

2. **Test for synonymy**: Do they mean the same/very similar thing?
   - Can you express them as one intensified word? -> Likely doublet
   - Would removing one change the meaning significantly? -> If no, doublet

3. **Check the relationship**:
   - Equal synonyms (both express same meaning)? -> figs-doublet
   - One modifies the other (adjective-like)? -> figs-hendiadys

4. **Check the level**:
   - Word/phrase level? -> figs-doublet
   - Full clauses with subjects and verbs? -> figs-parallelism

5. **Check for totality**:
   - Two ends of spectrum representing everything? -> figs-merism
   - Just emphasis/intensification? -> figs-doublet

---

## NOT figs-doublet

### Use figs-hendiadys when:
One word modifies/describes the other (not equal synonyms):
- "kingdom and glory" = glorious kingdom
- "answered and said" = responded (manner)
- "spirit and power" = powerful spirit

### Use figs-parallelism when:
Full clauses (subject + verb), not just words/phrases:
- "Praise Yahweh, all nations; / exalt him, all peoples"
- "He struck two men more righteous and better than himself" (if full parallel clauses)

### Use figs-merism when:
Two extremes representing totality:
- "young and old" = everyone (age spectrum)
- "heaven and earth" = all creation

### Use figs-reduplication when:
Same word repeated with intensifying force (especially Hebrew constructions):
- Infinitive absolute + finite verb ("dying, he died")

---

## Translation Strategies from TA

If doublet is not natural in target language:

1. **Translate only one word**: "false and deceptive words" -> "false things to say"

2. **Use one word with intensifier**: "scattered and dispersed" -> "very spread out"

3. **Use language's own emphasis method**: "without blemish and without spot" -> "without any blemish at all"

## AT Patterns

| Pattern | Example AT |
|---------|------------|
| Synonymous adjectives | "truly [adjective]" or "very [adjective]" |
| Synonymous nouns | Use one noun or combine: "everything he owns" |
| Synonymous verbs | Single intensified verb: "invited" for "sent and called" |
| Word repetition | Describe the emphasis: "called urgently" |
| Multiple synonyms | Single strong term: "completely destroy" |

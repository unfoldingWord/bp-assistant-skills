# translate-blessing

## Purpose
Identify blessings - short sayings asking God to do good for another person - that may not be recognized as blessings in their cultural form.

## Definition
Blessings are formulaic expressions where a speaker asks God (implicitly or explicitly) to do something good for another person. The speaker addresses the recipient directly but does not directly address God - it is understood that God hears and will act.

## Key Characteristics

1. **Directed to a person, not to God** - Speaker addresses the one receiving the blessing
2. **God is the understood actor** - Even when not explicitly named, God is expected to fulfill the blessing
3. **Often verbless in original languages** - "Grace to you" rather than "May grace be to you"
4. **Culturally specific forms** - What looks like a blessing in one culture may not be recognized in another

## Common Biblical Blessing Types

| Category | Example | Reference |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Letter Opening | "Grace to you and peace from God" | 1:2 pattern in most letters |
| Letter Closing | "The grace of the Lord Jesus be with you" | Letter closings |
| Letter Closing | "The Lord be with your spirit" | 2 Tim 4:22 |
| OT Greeting | "Yahweh be with you" | Ruth 2:4 |
| OT Greeting | "Peace be to you" / "Shalom to you" | Gen 43:23 |
| OT Greeting | "May Yahweh bless you" | Ruth 2:4 response |
| Pronouncement | "May you be the mother of thousands" | Gen 24:60 |
| Pronouncement | "Praised/Blessed be Yahweh" | Gen 9:26 |
| Pronouncement | "May the Lord give mercy to..." | 2 Tim 1:16 |
| Prayer-like | "May the Lord of peace himself give you peace" | 2 Thess 3:16 |
| Prayer-like | "May he prepare you in every good work" | Heb 13:21 |

## Recognition Triggers

Look for translate-blessing when:
- Verbless expressions of wish/desire addressed to a person: "Grace to you"
- Jussive/optative mood ("may X...") expressing desire for another's welfare
- Letter greetings and closings in epistles
- Greeting/farewell formulas in narrative
- "Blessed/Praised be [God/Yahweh]" expressions
- Peace/grace/mercy wished upon someone

## NOT This Issue

**Curses** - Opposite of blessings; wish harm rather than good
- Use appropriate issue based on curse type

**Prayers** - Direct address to God requesting something
- Blessings address the recipient; prayers address God

**Commands to bless** - "Bless Yahweh" as imperative
- May be figs-imperative or other grammatical issue

**Greeting words alone** - Simple "greet" without blessing content
- "They greeted him" = narrative description, not a blessing formula

**"Bless" meaning "say good things about"** (James 3:9-10)
- When "bless" means to speak well of someone (not invoke God's favor)
- This is closer to its literal meaning, not the blessing formula

## Confirmed Classifications (Issues Resolved)

- People across cultures generally know what a blessing is, so we can use that word in notes
- Focus notes on informing translators that the biblical character/author is speaking a blessing in a form they might not recognize
- Encourage translators to use a comparable blessing form from their own languages and cultures

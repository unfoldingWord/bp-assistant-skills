# figs-personification

## Purpose
Identify personification where non-living or abstract things are spoken of as if they could perform actions that only living beings can do.

## Definition
Personification speaks of something (abstract concept, object, natural phenomenon) as if it could do things that animals or people can do. The focus is on **agency** - the non-human thing performs actions.

This category includes:
- **Anthropomorphism**: Non-human things with human characteristics
- **Zoomorphism**: Things with animal characteristics

| Wisdom personified (Proverbs) | figs-personification | Capitalize "Wisdom" with feminine pronouns; lowercase "wisdom" with "it" for abstract concept |

| "face" = surface | figs-idiom | NOT personification |

## NOT Personification (Use These Instead)

### Use figs-metaphor for:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| X spoken of AS IF it were Y | Identity comparison, not agency |
| "house" = descendants | Topic-image relationship |
| "path/way" = behavior | Topic-image relationship |

### Use figs-idiom for:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| "struck to mouth of sword" | Cultural set phrase |
| "face" = surface | Fixed expression |
| "in the eyes of" | Common expression |

### Use figs-metonymy for:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| "hand" = power | Association, not agency |
| "name" = reputation | Association, not agency |
| Tribe name without personal action | Used by association only |

## Personification Categories

### 1. Abstract Concepts as Living Beings
Non-physical concepts performing human actions:

| Concept | Actions | Examples |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Sin | crouches, rules, reigns | "sin crouches at the door" |
| Death | rules, flees, as lord | "death ruled" "death flees from them" |
| Wisdom/Understanding | calls, gives voice, stations herself | "Does not Wisdom call out?" |
| Faith | is alive/dead, has works | "faith without works is dead" |
| Law/Scripture | speaks, says | "what does the scripture say" |
| Grace | rules, justifies | "grace might rule through righteousness" |
| Judgment | comes, is merciless | "the judgment is merciless" |
| Desire | conceives, gives birth | "desire having conceived bears sin" |
| Truth | is held back | "holding back the truth" |
| Conscience | bears witness | "their conscience bearing witness" |
| Kindness | leads | "the kindness of God leads you" |

### 2. Emotions/States as Moving or Acting
Internal experiences treated as external agents:

| Emotion | Actions | Examples |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Fear | falls on, comes upon, seizes | "fear fell on him" |
| Amazement | seizes, comes upon | "amazement seized them" |
| Wrath | comes, arrives | "your wrath has come" |
| Dread | comes | "when your dread comes" |
| Trouble | comes, touches | "trouble comes" |
| Rage | fills | "filled with rage" |
| Astonishment | comes upon | "astonishment came upon everyone" |

### 3. Communication/Information as Living
Messages and reports with agency:

| Item | Actions | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| Word/message | came to | "the word of God came to John" |
| Report/news | went out, spread | "news about him went out" |
| Voice | came from | "a voice came from heaven" |
| Cry | came to | "its cry has come to me" |
| Decree | went out | "a decree went out" |
| Joyful shout | comes into | "May a joyful shout not come into it" |

### 4. Natural Phenomena as Living
Elements of nature with agency:

| Phenomenon | Actions | Examples |
|------------|---------|----------|
| Day/Night | rules over, hastens | "to rule over the day" |
| Darkness/Shadow | claims, dwells | "May darkness claim it" |
| Cloud | dwells | "may a cloud dwell over it" |
| Fire | comes down, devours | "fire came down and devoured" |
| Heat/Frost | devours | "heat devoured me" |
| Disaster | overtakes | "the disaster will overtake me" |
| Earth | helps | "the earth helped the woman" |
| Leprosy | departs | "the leprosy departed from him" |
| Fever | leaves | "the fever left her" |

### 5. Objects/Concepts with Human Actions
Things performing actions:

| Object | Actions | Examples |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Box of Yahweh | came into | "the Box came into the camp" |
| Kingdom | stands, comes | "your kingdom will not stand" |
| Signs | come | "when these signs come to you" |
| Sword | makes childless | "your sword has made women childless" |
| Tongue | boasts | "the tongue boasts great things" |
| Battle/War | is strong against | "the battle was strong against" |
| Islands | flee | "every island fled" |
| Altar | speaks | "I heard the altar saying" |

### 6. Group as Individual Person
Collective entities treated as single persons:

| Group | Treatment | Examples |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Israel | as ancestor | "the God of Israel" representing Israelites |
| Tribes | speaking/acting | "Judah said to Simeon" |
| Cities | as women | "Ashdod - they will expel her" |
| Nations | acting | "Moab reproached" = Moabites reproached |

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify the subject**: Is it a non-living or abstract thing?

2. **Check the action**: Is the subject performing an action that requires agency (moving, speaking, ruling, seizing, coming, departing)?

3. **Verify it's not another issue**:
   - Is it a set cultural phrase? -> Check figs-idiom
   - Is it association (X represents Y)? -> Check figs-metonymy
   - Is it identity (X spoken of AS Y)? -> Check figs-metaphor

4. **Agency test**: The distinguishing feature is that the non-human thing is the **agent** performing an action. If the thing is merely being compared to something or standing for something, it's likely metaphor or metonymy instead.

## Common Patterns

### Typical Verbs Triggering Personification
- **Motion**: comes, goes, arrives, departs, flees, falls on, enters
- **Rule/Control**: rules, reigns, is lord over, controls
- **Speech**: says, speaks, calls out, gives voice
- **Physical action**: seizes, touches, devours, claims, dwells
- **Human states**: is alive, is dead, is merciless

### Typical Subjects
- Abstract qualities: faith, hope, love, wisdom, folly, sin, death, grace, truth
- Emotions: fear, dread, amazement, astonishment, rage, wrath
- Natural elements: day, night, darkness, fire, earth, wind
- Communications: word, voice, news, report, cry
- Groups treated as individuals: nations, tribes, cities

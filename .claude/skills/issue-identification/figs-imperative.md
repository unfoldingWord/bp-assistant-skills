# figs-imperative

## Purpose
Identify second-person imperative (command) forms used for purposes other than direct commands, including polite requests, conditions, divine performatives, encouragements, and invitations (for third-person imperatives, see [figs-imperative3p.md](figs-imperative3p.md)).

## Definition
An imperative is a verb form typically used for commands. However, in biblical literature, second-person imperatives are sometimes used for other purposes where the command form would be misunderstood if translated directly:

1. **Polite requests** - The speaker is asking, not commanding
2. **Conditions** - "Do X, and Y will happen" means "If you do X, then Y"
3. **Divine performatives** - God/Jesus causes something by commanding it
4. **Encouragement/assurance** - "Do not fear" means "You don't need to fear"
5. **Invitations** - "Follow me" means "I invite you to follow"

### Regular commands - NO NOTE NEEDED:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| "Go to Egypt" | Direct command, understood as command |
| "Love your neighbor" | Instruction, understood as instruction |
| "Do not steal" | Prohibition, understood as prohibition |

### Use writing-politeness for:
| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| "Behold me" (servant to master) | Courteous response to summons |

| Expression | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| "Behold me" (Yahweh announcing) | = "Now I am about to..." |

## Categories with Examples

### 1. Polite Requests (Most Common)
When someone addresses a superior (person, God) using command form but meaning a request.

**Markers:**
- Speaker is addressing someone of higher status
- Context shows speaker is asking, not ordering
- Often in prayers or appeals

| Example | Reference | Context |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| "Give us" | Matt 6:11 | prayer to God |
| "Forgive us" | Matt 6:12, Luke 11:4 | prayer to God |
| "Have mercy on me" | Luke 16:24, 18:13, 18:38 | appeals |
| "Send me to my master" | Gen 24:54 | servant's request |
| "Please swear" | Gen 21:23 | request from peer |
| "Vindicate me" | Luke 18:3 | appeal to judge |
| "Increase faith to us" | Luke 17:5 | disciples to Jesus |
| "Make me as one of your servants" | Luke 15:19 | prodigal son |

### 2. Conditions (Proverbs Pattern)
When an imperative introduces a condition: "Do X, and Y happens" = "If X, then Y"

**Markers:**
- Imperative + result clause
- Found especially in Proverbs
- Teaching what happens IF someone does something

| Example | Reference |
|---------|-----------|
| "Discipline your son, and he will cause you to rest" | Prov 29:17 |
| "Rebuke a wise one, and he will love you" | Prov 9:8 |
| "Give to a wise one, and he will become wiser" | Prov 9:9 |
| "Do not abandon wisdom and she will watch over you" | Prov 4:6 |
| "Teach a child the way he should go, and when he is old..." | Prov 22:6 |
| "Dispute your dispute" (introduces conditional situation) | Prov 25:9 |

### 3. Divine Performatives
When God/Jesus commands something and the command itself causes it to happen.

**Markers:**
- Speaker is God or Jesus
- Object cannot obey on their own
- Command causes the result

| Example | Reference | Effect |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| "Be clean" | Matt 8:3, Luke 5:13 | healing leprosy |
| "Get up" / "Arise" | Luke 5:24, 7:14, 8:54 | healing/raising dead |
| "Stretch out your hand" | Luke 6:10 | enabling healing |
| "Let there be light" | Gen 1:3 | creation |
| "Let it be done" | Matt 8:13, 9:29 | granting requests |

### 4. Encouragement/Assurance
When "Do not X" means "You don't need to X" or "There's no reason to X"

**Markers:**
- Negative command form
- Context shows speaker is reassuring, not prohibiting
- Often "Do not fear/be afraid"

| Example | Reference | Context |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| "Do not be afraid" | Luke 1:13, 1:30, 2:10 | angel reassuring |

### 5. Invitations
When a command form is actually an invitation or offer.

**Markers:**
- Speaker is offering, not ordering
- Addressee has choice whether to accept
- Often discipleship calls

| Example | Reference | Context |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| "Follow me" | Luke 5:27 | Jesus inviting Levi |
| "Say it, Teacher" | Luke 7:40 | inviting Jesus to speak |

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify imperative form**: Is there a command/prohibition verb form?

2. **Check if it's a regular command**: Can it be understood naturally as a command?
   - If yes: NO NOTE NEEDED
   - If no: Continue to step 3

3. **Determine the function**:

   | Question | If YES | Type |
   |----------|--------|------|
   | Is speaker addressing a superior? | Could be request | figs-imperative,request |
   | Does imperative + result = condition? | Conditional | figs-imperative,condition |
   | Is God/Jesus causing something by commanding? | Performative | figs-imperative |
   | Is "do not X" meant as reassurance? | Encouragement | figs-imperative |
   | Is speaker offering/inviting? | Invitation | figs-imperative |
   | Is it "Let + 3rd person"? | Third-person | **figs-imperative3p** (see separate skill) |

4. **Check existing patterns**: Look for similar expressions in processed notes

## Critical Distinctions

### figs-imperative vs. figs-idiom:
- "Arise" (= take action, get going) -> **figs-idiom** (fixed expression)
- "Arise" (Jesus raising the dead) -> **figs-imperative** (divine performative)

### figs-imperative vs. writing-politeness:
- "Please give us bread" -> **figs-imperative,request** (command form issue)
- "Behold me" (servant responding) -> **writing-politeness** (courteous formula)

### figs-imperative vs. figs-declarative:
- "Give us our bread" (command form for request) -> **figs-imperative**
- "You shall not murder" (future form for instruction) -> **figs-declarative**

### When NO note is needed:
- Regular commands understood as commands
- Instructions understood as instructions
- Prohibitions understood as prohibitions

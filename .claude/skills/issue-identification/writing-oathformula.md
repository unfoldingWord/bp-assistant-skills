# writing-oathformula

## Definition

An oath is a solemn promise or testimony that invokes divine punishment if broken. Oath formulas in biblical text have up to four parts:
1. Calling on God (or a sacred thing) to witness and judge
2. Making the promise (may be implied)
3. Stating what would violate the oath (may be implied)
4. Describing the punishment if violated (often unstated due to fear of its power)

## Two Types

### 1. Conditional Oath
Speaker states first part of condition ("if I do not...") without the consequence.
- "I raise my hand to Yahweh... if from a thread even to the strap of a sandal..."
- "May Yahweh do thus to me, and thus may he add, if death separates between me and between you"
- "Thus may God do and thus may he add"

### 2. Guarantee Oath
Speaker swears by something sacred to guarantee truth/promise without stating a condition.
- "The life of Yahweh" (most common) - "I swear by Yahweh"
- "The life of your soul" - swearing by the listener's life
- "The life of Yahweh and the life of your soul" - combining two sacred things
- "The truth of Christ is in me" (NT equivalent)
- "I adjure you by..." - placing another under oath

## Recognition Triggers

**Hebrew patterns:**
- "hay-YHWH" (the life of Yahweh) - guarantee formula
- "hay nepes-ka" (the life of your soul) - guarantee by listener's life
- "ko ya'aseh..." (thus may God do...) - conditional with unstated punishment
- "haremoti yadi el-YHWH" (I raise my hand to Yahweh) - oath gesture
- "hishba'ti" (I adjure) - placing someone under oath

**Greek patterns:**
- "horkizo se ton theon" (I make you swear by God)
- "estin aletheia Christou en emoi" (the truth of Christ is in me)

## When to Use

Use writing-oathformula when:
1. Speaker invokes deity or sacred thing to guarantee words
2. "The life of Yahweh" or "the life of your soul" appears
3. "Thus may God/Yahweh do to me and add" pattern appears
4. Hand-raising or other oath gestures are described with divine invocation
5. "I adjure you by..." appears
6. NT pattern "the truth of Christ is in me" or similar

## NOT This Issue

- Simple vows ("I vow to do X") without invoking divine judgment - use figs-explicit
- Blessings ("May God bless you") without oath structure - use translate-blessing
- Curses on others without self-obligation - different category
- Promises without sacred invocation - not an oath formula

## Translation Challenges

1. **Missing parts**: Biblical speakers often omit punishment clause for fear it would cause it to happen
2. **Cultural unfamiliarity**: Some cultures don't practice oath-swearing
3. **Formulaic language**: "The life of Yahweh" is not about living/dying but about swearing
4. **Elliptical conditions**: "if I do X" implies "then may God punish me"

## Categories from Examples

| Pattern | Example | Type |
|---------|---------|------|
| hay-YHWH | "The life of Yahweh" | Guarantee |
| hay-YHWH + title | "The life of Yahweh of Armies" | Guarantee |
| hay napseka | "The life of your soul, O king" | Guarantee |
| Combined | "The life of Yahweh and the life of your soul" | Double guarantee |
| ko ya'aseh | "Thus may Yahweh do to me" | Conditional (unstated punishment) |
| Hand raising | "I raise my hand to Yahweh" | Gesture + oath |
| Adjure | "I adjure you, daughters of Jerusalem" | Placing under oath |
| NT formula | "The truth of Christ is in me" | Guarantee |

## Genres Where Common

- **OT Narrative**: Most common (1 Samuel has 21 occurrences)
- **Poetry**: Song of Songs uses "I adjure you" repeatedly
- **Wisdom**: Job uses oath formulas to assert truth
- **NT Epistles**: Paul uses oath-like formulas (2 Cor 11:10)
- **Gospels**: Less common but present (Mark 5:7)

# figs-you

## Purpose
Identify when a translation note should clarify who "you" refers to or provide guidance about forms of "you" that doesn't fit the more specific figs-you* categories.

## Definition (from Translation Academy)
The TA article "Forms of You" is a hub article that introduces several distinctions:
- **Singular vs Plural** - One person vs multiple people
- **Dual** - Exactly two people (some languages)
- **Formal vs Informal** - Based on relationship/status
- **Masculine vs Feminine** - Based on gender of addressee

It links to more specific articles (figs-yousingular, figs-youdual, figs-youformal, figs-youcrowd) for detailed guidance.

## When to Use figs-you (Base Article)

Use figs-you when:
1. **Clarifying WHO "you" refers to** - Not just singular/plural, but identity
2. **"You" refers to a specific group** - Jews, Pharisees, disciples, etc.
3. **General plural clarification with group identity** - More than just "plural"
4. **Mixed audiences** - When "you" shifts between different groups

## Confirmed Classifications

### USE figs-you when:

1. **Group identity clarification**
   - "The word **you** is plural and refers to the Jewish leaders"
   - "Here **you** refers to the Samaritan people"
   - "**You** is plural and refers to the disciples to whom Jesus is speaking"

2. **Emphasis or explicit pronoun**
   - "**You yourselves** - Here, **You** is plural and refers to all the people"
   - "Jesus is using the plural form of **you** to indicate he is speaking to all"

3. **Shifting audiences**
   - "In this verse every occurrence of **you** is plural and refers to the disciples"
   - First "you" is singular (to Peter), second is plural (to all)

4. **Ambiguous reference needs clarification**
   - "The word **you** is plural here. This means Jesus was not only speaking to the royal official, but also to the other people who were there"

### Key Pattern in Notes:
The standard formula often includes group identity:
> "Here **you** is plural and refers to [specific group]. If it would be helpful in your language, you could state this explicitly. Alternate translation: [you + group identifier]"

## NOT This Issue (Use Instead)

| Situation | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Simple singular/plural without group ID | figs-yousingular |
| Exactly two people | figs-youdual |
| Singular addressing a crowd | figs-youcrowd |
| Formal vs informal register | figs-youformal |
| Clarifying pronoun referent (he/she/they) | writing-pronouns |

## Common Patterns from Published Notes

1. **Gospel of John** - Highest concentration (23 occurrences)
   - Jesus speaking to various Jewish groups
   - Shifting between Pharisees, disciples, crowds
   - "you Jews" / "you Jewish authorities" / "you disciples"

2. **Luke** - Second highest (42 occurrences)
   - Similar gospel teaching contexts
   - Parables with audience clarification

3. **Epistles** - Clarifying church vs individuals
   - "you" meaning the whole church
   - "you" meaning specific subgroups

4. **Old Testament dialogues** - Identifying parties
   - Prophetic addresses to nations
   - Conversations with identified groups

## Alternate Translations

Alternate translations for figs-you typically:
- Add group identifier: "you Jewish leaders" / "you disciples"
- Make explicit: "all of you here" / "you who are my disciples"
- Clarify scope: "you Judeans" / "you Samaritan people"

## Key Distinction from figs-yousingular

| figs-you | figs-yousingular |
|----------|------------------|
| Focus on WHO is addressed | Focus on NUMBER (sg/pl) |
| Identifies specific group | Just indicates singular or plural |
| Often provides explicit naming | Often just states "plural/singular" |
| Used when identity matters | Used when number matters |

## Biblical Examples

**John 3:11** (ULT)
> "but **you** do not receive"

Note: "The word **you** is plural and could refer to: (1) the Jewish people in general. Alternate translation: [you Jews]; (2) Nicodemus and his fellow Jewish leaders. Alternate translation: [you Jewish leaders do not welcome]"

**John 4:22** (ULT)
> "**You** worship what you do not know"

Note: "**You** is plural here in this verse and refers to the Samaritan people. If it would be helpful in your language, you could state this explicitly. Alternate translation: [you Samaritan people]"

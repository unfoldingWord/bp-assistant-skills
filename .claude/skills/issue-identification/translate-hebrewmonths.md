# translate-hebrewmonths

## Purpose
Identify references to Hebrew calendar months that translators may need help understanding or expressing.

## Definition
The Hebrew calendar has twelve months beginning in spring (March-April). Months are referenced either by name (Aviv, Ziv, Sivan, Nisan, Adar, etc.) or by ordinal number ("the first month," "the seventh month"). Readers may:
- Not recognize Hebrew month names
- Not realize "the first month" refers to the Hebrew calendar (starting in spring)
- Not know what season a month occurs in

## When to Use

### IS translate-hebrewmonths
- **Named months**: Aviv, Nisan, Ziv, Sivan, Tammuz, Ab, Elul, Ethanim, Bul, Kislev, Tebeth, Shebat, Adar
- **Ordinal month references**: "the first month," "the seventh month," "the ninth month" (referring to Hebrew calendar)
- **Day + month combinations**: "on the tenth day of the seventh month," "the twenty-fourth of the ninth"

### NOT translate-hebrewmonths
- **"Days/months/years" meaning a period of time**: Use figs-metonymy (e.g., "all the days of Joshua" = his lifetime, "the month of your rejoicing" = time when you rejoice)
- **"Day" meaning occasion**: Use figs-idiom (e.g., "the day of uncleanness" = on the occasion of)
- **Generic time references**: "many months," "after several months" (no specific Hebrew month)

## Hebrew Month Reference

| Month | Number | Western Equivalent | Season |
|-------|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Aviv/Nisan | 1st | late March-early April | Spring (late rains, harvest begins) |
| Ziv | 2nd | late April-early May | Harvest |
| Sivan | 3rd | late May-early June | End of harvest, dry season begins |
| Tammuz | 4th | late June-early July | Dry season |
| Ab | 5th | late July-early August | Dry season |
| Elul | 6th | late August-early September | End of dry season |
| Ethanim | 7th | late September-early October | Early rains |
| Bul | 8th | late October-early November | Plowing and sowing |
| Kislev | 9th | late November-early December | End of sowing, cold begins |
| Tebeth | 10th | late December-early January | Cold, possible snow |
| Shebat | 11th | late January-early February | Coldest, heavy rainfall |
| Adar | 12th | late February-early March | Cold season |

**Note**: Aviv was called Nisan after the Babylonian exile.

## Recognition Patterns

1. **Hebrew month names appearing in text**
   - "in the month of Nisan" (Neh 2:1)
   - "in the month of Kislev" (Neh 1:1)
   - "the month of Adar" (Esther, 8x)
   - "in the month of Bul" (1 Kings 6:38)

2. **Ordinal + month**
   - "the first month" / "the seventh month" / "the ninth month"
   - Often with "of the Hebrew calendar" implied

3. **Day + ordinal month**
   - "on the first day of the seventh month"
   - "on the twenty-fourth of the ninth"

## Note Patterns from Published TNs

**For named months**:
- "**Nisan** is the name of the first month of the Hebrew calendar."
- "**Adar** is the name of the twelfth and last month of the Hebrew calendar. See how you translated this in [previous verse]."
- "**Kislev** is the ninth month of the Hebrew calendar."

**For ordinal months**:
- "The **seventh month** of the Hebrew calendar includes August and September on Western calendars."
- "This refers to the first month of the Hebrew calendar, which occurs in March-April on modern calendars."
- "You could convert the Hebrew **month** into an equivalent on the calendar that your culture uses. However, the Jews used a lunar calendar, so if you use a solar calendar, the equivalency will be different every year."

**For repeated references**: Link back to first occurrence ("See how you decided to represent a Hebrew month in [verse]")

## Key Considerations

1. **Lunar vs Solar calendars**: Hebrew calendar is lunar; direct conversion to solar calendars gives different dates each year
2. **Season is often more important than exact date**: Knowing "spring harvest" or "autumn rains" may help more than "late March"
3. **Consistency within book**: Use "See how you translated" pattern for repeated month names
4. **Pre/Post-exile names**: Aviv = Nisan (same month, different name periods)

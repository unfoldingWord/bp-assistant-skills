# figs-metonymy

## Purpose
Identify metonymy in biblical text, distinguishing it from metaphor, synecdoche, and idiom.

## Definition
Metonymy is a figure of speech where an item (physical or abstract) is called not by its own name, but by the name of something closely **associated** with it. The key is ASSOCIATION, not comparison (metaphor) or part-whole relationship (synecdoche).

## The Metonymy Test
**Association Test**: Does item X represent item Y because X is commonly associated with Y?
- "blood" represents death (blood is associated with violent death)
- "throne" represents royal authority (throne is associated with kingship)
- "hand" represents power (hand is associated with action/control)

**Compare with:**
- **Metaphor**: X spoken of AS IF it were Y (comparison, not association)
- **Synecdoche**: Part represents whole, OR whole represents part (substitution test: can you swap them?)
- **Idiom**: Fixed cultural expression with non-compositional meaning

## Synecdoche vs Metonymy Test

From Issues Resolved: "Sometimes there is an admittedly fine line between synecdoche and metonymy"

**Substitution Test**: If the whole can substitute for the part, it's synecdoche.
- "they did not consult the mouth of Yahweh" = "they did not consult Yahweh" -> **synecdoche**
- "Yahweh gave them into the hand of the Philistines" =/= "Yahweh gave them into the Philistines" -> **metonymy**

**Association vs Part-Whole**:
- Does X represent Y because X is **part of** Y? -> synecdoche
- Does X represent Y because X is **associated with** Y? -> metonymy

## Metonymy Categories with Examples

### Body Part Metonyms
| Term | Represents | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **hand** | power, control, agency | "into your hand" = "into your power" |
| **hand** | agency/through | "by the hand of Moses" = "through Moses" |
| **face** | presence | "before his face" = "in his presence" |
| **ears** | hearing | "open your ears" = "listen" |
| **right hand** | place of honor | "at the right hand of God" |

### Physical Object Metonyms
| Term | Represents | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **blood** | death (violent) | "the blood of Jesus" = "Jesus' death" |
| **cup** | contents (wine) | "this cup" = "the wine in this cup" |
| **throne** | royal authority | "on the throne" = "ruling as king" |
| **table** | feast/meal | "the king's table" = "the king's feast" |
| **sword** | warfare/military power | "by the sword" = "by warfare" |

### Death/Battle Metonyms
| Term | Represents | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **fallen** | died (in battle) | "the mighty have fallen" = "the mighty have died" |
| **by the sword** | in battle | "fallen by the sword" = "died in battle" |
| **blood** | death/guilt | "his blood is on his head" = "responsible for his death" |

**Note:** These are metonymy (association), not euphemism (politeness).
- "fallen" - soldiers fall when mortally wounded (association)
- Contrast with "fell asleep" - polite way to say died (euphemism)

### Abstract/Concept Metonyms
| Term | Represents | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **name** | reputation, character | "for the name of Yahweh" = "for Yahweh's reputation" |
| **name** | the person himself | "in the name of Jesus" = "representing Jesus" |
| **word** | message, teaching | "the word of God" = "God's message" |
| **wrath** | punishment | "flee the wrath" = "flee the punishment" |
| **grace** | gospel message | "the grace of God" = "God's gracious message" |

### Time Metonyms
| Term | Represents | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **days** | time period, lifetime | "all his days" = "throughout his lifetime" |
| **days** | era, time of | "in the days of Noah" = "in Noah's time" |

### Group/Place Metonyms
| Term | Represents | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **world** | people in the world | "the world hates you" = "ungodly people hate you" |
| **world** | ungodly value system | "do not love the world" |
| **gates** | community within | "within your gates" = "in your community" |
| Tribe names | tribe's people/army | "Judah" = "the people of Judah" |
| Nation names | nation's people | "Israel" = "the Israelites" |

These are confirmed metonymy by the content team (close association, not comparison):

- FACE represents presence, sight, knowledge, perception, attention, judgment, front, or surface
- HAND represents power, control, agency, or action
- HEAD represents tip, top, or uppermost part
- HEART represents thinking, feeling, or attitude
- EYES represent sight, knowledge, perception, attention, or judgment
- HEAD represents a ruler or leader
- MOUTH represents speech or words
- NAME represents the person, or their power/authority/position/status
- NOSE represents anger
- TONGUE represents the language spoken
- BRONZE represents strength

Also note:
- "in the EYES of" = idiom (not metonymy)
- LYING DOWN or SLEEPING means dying or being dead = euphemism (not metonymy)

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify potential figure**: Is something called by a different name?

2. **Check Issues Resolved list**: Is this expression already classified?

3. **Apply the Association Test**:
   - Is X associated with Y (blood with death, throne with kingship)?
   - NOT: Is X part of Y (synecdoche) or X compared to Y (metaphor)?

4. **Apply the Substitution Test** (for body parts):
   - Can the whole person substitute? -> synecdoche
   - Cannot substitute? -> metonymy

5. **Check for fixed expressions**: If the phrase is a set cultural expression with fixed meaning, it may be idiom instead.

## Common Pitfalls

1. **"In the eyes of"** - This is idiom, not metonymy (per Issues Resolved)
2. **"From the face" (causal)** - When meaning "because of," use idiom
3. **"Struck"** - When meaning "defeated/killed," use figs-explicit, not metonymy
4. **Heart** - Usually metaphor (inner person spoken of AS thoughts/feelings)
5. **Body parts where whole substitutes** - These are synecdoche, not metonymy

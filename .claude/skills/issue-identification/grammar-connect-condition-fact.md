# grammar-connect-condition-fact

## Purpose
Identify conditions where "if" really means "since" or "because" - the condition is actually TRUE, not hypothetical.

## Definition
A factual condition is a conditional statement where the speaker KNOWS the condition is TRUE. "If" functions as "since" or "because." The conditional form is used rhetorically, not to express uncertainty.

---

## Four Condition Types

| Issue | Use For | Key Test |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **grammar-connect-condition-fact** | "If" meaning "since" | Condition is actually TRUE |
| **grammar-connect-condition-contrary** | Counterfactual arguments | Speaker KNOWS condition is FALSE |
| **grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical** | Simple conditions | REAL possible condition, grammar unclear |
| **figs-hypo** | Imaginary scenarios | UNREAL scenario for illustration |

---

## Categories

### 1. "If" Meaning "Since/Because" (Known Fact)
**Pattern**: "if [TRUE FACT], then [CONSEQUENCE]"

| Expression | The speaker KNOWS |
|------------|-------------------|
| "If God is for us, who can be against us?" (Rom 8:31) | God IS for believers |
| "if, being enemies, we were reconciled" (Rom 5:10) | They WERE enemies |
| "if children, also heirs" (Rom 8:17) | Believers ARE children |
| "If you, being a Jew, live like a Gentile" (Gal 2:14) | Peter DOES live like a Gentile |
| "if God so clothes the grass of the field" (Matt 6:30) | God DOES clothe the grass |
| "if I cast out demons by the Spirit of God" (Matt 12:28) | He DOES cast out by the Spirit |

### 2. "If" for Things That WILL/DO Happen
**Pattern**: "if [CERTAIN EVENT], then..."

| Expression | The speaker KNOWS |
|------------|-------------------|
| "if someone might see you eating" (1 Cor 8:10) | Someone WILL see at some point |
| "if Timothy comes" (1 Cor 16:10) | Timothy has been sent; arrival is certain |
| "if anyone says to you, 'Look, here is the Christ'" (Matt 24:23) | People WILL say this |
| "if food causes my brother to stumble" (1 Cor 8:13) | This DOES/WILL happen |

### 3. "If" to Identify a Group or Class
**Pattern**: "if anyone [DESCRIPTION]..." = "whoever [DESCRIPTION]..."

| Expression | The speaker KNOWS |
|------------|-------------------|
| "If anyone thinks himself to be a prophet" (1 Cor 14:37) | Some DO think this |
| "If anyone does not love the Lord" (1 Cor 16:22) | Some DON'T love the Lord |
| "if anyone is ignorant" (1 Cor 14:38) | Some ARE ignorant |
| "if any other commandment" (Rom 13:9) | There ARE other commandments |

### 4. "If" in Rhetorical Arguments (Assumed Truth)
**Pattern**: "if [ACCEPTED PREMISE], then [CONCLUSION]"

| Expression | The speaker KNOWS |
|------------|-------------------|
| "if Christ is preached that he has been raised" (1 Cor 15:12) | Christ IS preached as raised |
| "if by the trespass of the one..." (Rom 5:15, 17) | Adam's trespass DID bring death |
| "if anyone builds on the foundation" (1 Cor 3:12) | People ARE building |

---

## NOT grammar-connect-condition-fact

### Use grammar-connect-condition-contrary for:
**Key signals**: "so-called", "supposed", "even if", or explicit false-marking

| Expression | Why NOT fact |
|------------|--------------|
| "even if there are **so-called** gods" (1 Cor 8:5) | "so-called" marks as FALSE |
| "If God were your Father, you would love me" | Condition is FALSE - contrary argument |
| "If you had known, you would not have condemned" | Past counterfactual |
| "If there is no resurrection" (1 Cor 15) | Paul knows there IS resurrection |

### Use grammar-connect-condition-hypothetical for:
| Expression | Why NOT fact |
|------------|--------------|
| "A man, when he has..." | Real possible condition, needs "if" clarification |
| Missing "if" or "then" | Grammar issue, condition may or may not be true |

### Use figs-hypo for:
| Expression | Why NOT fact |
|------------|--------------|
| "If only we had died" | Imaginary wish/regret |
| "Suppose someone said to God..." | Teaching scenario, not real |

---

## Recognition Process

1. **Identify the condition**: Is there an "if...then" structure?
2. **Check the speaker's certainty**: Does the speaker KNOW the condition is TRUE?
3. **Check if "if" = "since/because"**: Can you replace "if" with "since"?

**Key Test**: Does the speaker know the "if" part is actually TRUE?

---

## Linguistic Markers

### Greek
- First-class conditional (ei + indicative) often indicates factual condition
- Context determines whether condition is assumed true

### Hebrew
- 'im (if) can introduce factual conditions
- Context and rhetorical structure determine meaning

# ULT (unfoldingWord Literal Text) Guidelines

Reference document extracted from the [Gateway Language Manual](https://gl-manual.readthedocs.io/en/latest/gl_guidelines.html) and [Appendix A: Style Sheet](https://gl-manual.readthedocs.io/en/latest/gl_appendixA.html).

## Core Principles

The ULT should **follow the original language literally** to give users insight into the original form. Exceptions apply when:
- Literal translation would produce **wrong meaning**
- English would be **ungrammatical and incomprehensible**

The ULT helps users understand **what the original says** rather than what it means (that's UST's role).

## Vocabulary & Style

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| No contractions | "do not" not "don't" |
| Use common, translatable vocabulary | Prefer simple words over rare ones |
| Differentiate from UST | Use different vocabulary than UST to help translators see options |
| Formal, established usage | Prefer formal over informal English |
| Avoid split infinitives | "to understand clearly" not "to clearly understand" |
| Use "whom" not "who" (formal) | "the man whom you saw" |

## Capitalization

### General Rules
- Follow **2011 NIV** practice
- Capitalize titles: "Son of Man", "King David", "the Messiah"
- "Scripture(s)" capitalized for the entire Bible; lowercase for individual passages
- "Word" capitalized when referring to the Bible or Jesus ("the Living Word")

### Pronouns for God
Lowercase except at sentence start and "I" (always capitalized).

### After Punctuation
- After colon: Capitalize only if starting a complete sentence
- After dash: Capitalize only proper nouns

## Numbers

| Range | Format | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1-10 | Spell out | "one", "five", "ten" |
| 11+ | Numerals | "11", "150", "3,000" |
| Titles | Spell out | "the Twelve (apostles)" |
| Dates | Cardinals | "April 17" not "April 17th" |
| Ranges | En-dash | "30-40 people" |

## Quotation Marks

- Use **only at beginning and ending** of direct speech
- Do NOT put quotes at the start of each verse in continuous speech
- American English: double quotes first, single quotes for nested
- Periods and commas go **inside** quotation marks
- Colons and semicolons go **outside** quotation marks
- Unspoken thoughts: Use quotation marks (Bible convention)

## Punctuation

### Oxford Comma
Use the serial comma: "red, white, **and** blue"

### Dashes
- En-dash (-): Number ranges, no spaces (30-40)
- Em-dash (--): Interruptions or parenthetical material, use sparingly

### Apostrophes & Possessives
- Singular: Add 's (father's house)
- Classical/biblical names (2+ syllables ending in s): apostrophe only (Jesus' disciples)
- Single-syllable names: 's (Zeus's warnings)
- Plural ending in s: apostrophe only (priests' robes)

## Word Order & Grammar

- May **differ from Hebrew/Greek** when needed for clear English
- Grammar should be **standard English** while reflecting original structure
- Don't sacrifice meaning for literalness
- Grammar may reflect translation issues in source languages

## Curly Brackets {supplied words}

Curly brackets mark words **added for English grammar** that are not in the original text.

**Use brackets for:**
- Words required by English grammar: "while they {were} eating" (genitive absolute)
- Implied subjects/objects when needed for clarity: "{it was} good"

**Do NOT use brackets for:**
- Copulas (be verbs) with participles: "Yahweh is giving" not "Yahweh {is} giving"
- Words within the semantic range of the original: "hill country" not "hill {country}"

## Spelling

Follow **American English**:
- color (not colour)
- curb (not kerb)
- worshiped (not worshipped)
- program (not programme)

## Spacing

- **Single space** between sentences (not double)
- No extra spaces at paragraph start/end

## Avoiding Common Issues

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| Contractions | Full forms |
| & (ampersand) | "and" |
| Informal "they" (singular) | "he" or restructure |
| Preposition at end | Restructure when possible |
| Split infinitives | Move adverb |

## Conjunctions

- Avoid starting sentences with conjunctions when possible
- Maintain parallel structure in compound constructions
- "Not only...but also" requires parallel elements

## Formal Register

The ULT maintains a **formal register**:
- Use subjunctive mood ("If he were..." not "If he was...")
- Use adverbs properly ("She spoke quietly" not "She spoke quiet")
- Use "whom" as object pronoun

# Zechariah — Reference Notes

## Structure of Chapters 1–6 (Visions and Oracles)

Source: David J. Clark, "Vision and Oracle in Zechariah 1–6" in *Biblical Hebrew and Discourse Linguistics*.

### Macro-structure

The main sections of chapters 1–8 are marked by dates at 1:1, 1:7, and 7:1.

The eight visions (1:7–6:8) are framed by two additional messages:
- **Introductory oracle**: 1:1–6 (before the visions)
- **Concluding oracle**: 6:9–15 (after the visions)

### Chiastic Pattern of the Eight Visions (A-B-C-B-A)

The visions follow a three-layer chiasm:

- **Outer layer** (A): Visions 1 and 8 — the Lord's concern for the world at large
- **Middle layer** (B): Visions 2–3 (paired) and Visions 6–7 (paired) — the welfare of the Lord's people
- **Inner layer** (C): Visions 4 and 5 — the leadership of the Lord's people

### Oracles Embedded Within Visions

- 1:14–17 — embedded in Vision 1 (first vision)
- 3:6–10 — embedded in Vision 4 (fourth vision)
- 4:6b–10a — embedded in Vision 5 (fifth vision)

An additional independent oracle is placed **between** Visions 3 and 4: Zech 2:6–13.

### Summary of Oracle Placement

| Passage    | Type                          |
|------------|-------------------------------|
| 1:1–6      | Introductory oracle           |
| 1:14–17    | Oracle embedded in Vision 1   |
| 2:6–13     | Oracle between Visions 3 and 4|
| 3:6–10     | Oracle embedded in Vision 4   |
| 4:6b–10a   | Oracle embedded in Vision 5   |
| 6:9–15     | Concluding oracle             |

## Rhetorical Devices

### Inclusion (Envelope Structure)

Example in 1:14–17: the angel's message opens with "I am exceedingly jealous for Jerusalem and for Zion" (v. 14) and closes with "the Lord will again comfort Zion and again choose Jerusalem" (v. 17). The place names form an envelope around the message, and they also appear in **chiastic (reversed) order**: Jerusalem … Zion … Zion … Jerusalem.
